## Angle

Orange wine is the fourth wine colour — and the oldest winemaking technique still in active use. It's made from white grapes but treated like red wine: the skins stay in contact with the juice, building colour, tannin, and a savoury depth you don't find in white or rosé. Georgia has been making it this way for 8,000 years. The modern revival started in Friuli and Slovenia in the 1990s.

The key reader payoff: understanding orange wine means you'll know exactly who it's for (food people, savoury tasters, adventurous drinkers) and whether that's you.

## Key content requirements

- What orange wine is: white grapes + extended skin contact = colour, tannin, oxidative character
- Technique history: Georgia 8,000+ years, qvevri clay vessels, UNESCO heritage
- Modern revival: Friuli-Venezia Giulia (Italy), Slovenia, Josko Gravner as pioneer
- How it differs from white, rosé, red: the quadrant explanation
- Colour spectrum: golden amber to deep copper-orange
- Taste profile: tannic for a white, savoury, oxidative notes (dried fruit, nuts, tea, honey), bone-dry
- Main regions: Georgia, Friuli/Italy, Slovenia, Croatia, California, Australia, Jura (France)
- Key grape varieties: Pinot Grigio, Gewurztraminer, Ribolla Gialla (Italy), Rkatsiteli (Georgia), Pinot Gris, Muscat
- Food pairings: most food-friendly wine category — bold flavours, rich dishes, spiced foods, aged cheeses
- Serving temperature: cooler than red (12-14°C / 54-57°F), warmer than white
- "Will I like it?" test: if you like tannins in red, enjoy savoury/umami flavours, or find white wine too light — yes
