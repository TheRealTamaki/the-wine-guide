### Key content to cover
**Fortification process:**
- Adding neutral grape spirit (brandy) during or after fermentation
- Added during fermentation = retains residual sugar (sweet styles: Ruby Port, tawny, PX)
- Added after fermentation = dry styles (fino Sherry, dry Vermouth, Sercial Madeira)
- ABV range: 15–22% vs table wine 11–14%

**Major styles:**
- **Port** (Portugal, Douro Valley): ruby (fresh, fruity, 3 years), tawny (barrel-aged, nutty, 10/20/40yr), vintage (single year, 20+ years in bottle), LBV (late-bottled vintage, ready to drink), white Port (dry to off-dry, serve chilled)
- **Sherry** (Spain, Jerez/Sherry Triangle): fino (bone dry, light, serve cold), manzanilla (fino from Sanlúcar, saline), amontillado (aged fino, nutty, medium), oloroso (full-bodied, dark, dry to off-dry), Pedro Ximénez/PX (intensely sweet, dried fig, raisin), cream (blended sweet)
- **Madeira** (Portuguese island): heat-stabilised (estufagem process), virtually indestructible once opened; styles by grape: Sercial (dry), Verdelho (off-dry), Bual/Boal (medium-sweet), Malmsey (rich sweet)
- **Marsala** (Sicily, Italy): dry, semi-dry, sweet; cooking vs drinking; Chicken Marsala connection
- **Vermouth** (France/Italy): aromatised fortified; dry/white (cocktails, Martini), sweet/red (Negroni, Manhattan); serve over ice with orange
- **Banyuls** (Roussillon, France): French answer to Port, Grenache-based, great with chocolate
- **Mavrodaphne** (Greece): sweet red from Patras, dried fruit and spice

**Serving temps:**
- Fino/manzanilla Sherry: fridge-cold (7–9°C), small pour, wine glass
- Ruby/vintage Port: room temp or very slightly cool (16–18°C)
- Tawny Port: lightly chilled (12–14°C)
- Madeira: room temp or lightly chilled
- Vermouth: over ice with a garnish

**Opened bottle longevity (key differentiator vs table wine):**
- Fino Sherry: 1 week in fridge (treat like white wine)
- Ruby Port: 4–6 weeks (stopper in, cool dark place)
- Tawny Port: 2–3 months (already oxidised)
- Madeira: months to years (heat-stabilised, nearly indestructible)
- Vintage Port: drink within 2–3 days of opening
- Vermouth: 1 month in fridge

**Pairings:**
- Ruby Port: dark chocolate, blue cheese, walnuts
- Tawny Port: crème brûlée, pecan pie, dried fruit
- Fino Sherry: Jamón, olives, almonds, fried fish
- PX: ice cream, chocolate cake, stilton
- Madeira: mushroom dishes, aged cheeses, foie gras
- Marsala: mushrooms, chicken, cream sauces
- Banyuls: dark chocolate (classic)

**Fortified vs dessert wine:**
- Key distinction: fortified = spirit added; dessert wine = sweet through late harvest/botrytis/drying
- Some fortified wines (fino Sherry, dry Vermouth) are bone dry — the category is not synonymous with sweet
- Examples: Tokaji (dessert, not fortified), Sauternes (dessert, not fortified)
