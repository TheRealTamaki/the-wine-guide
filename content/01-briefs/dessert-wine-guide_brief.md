## Article Structure

### Intro
- QuickAnswer: direct answer to "what is dessert wine?"
- Hook: scenario — you ordered something sweet at a restaurant and the dry red you're drinking suddenly tasted sharp and bitter. That's the sweetness mismatch. This article fixes that.
- 5 fascination bullets

### H2s (as questions for AI search coverage)

1. **How Is Dessert Wine Made?** — Cover the 4 production methods: late harvest, noble rot/botrytis, ice wine (eiswein), dried grape/passito. Fortification is a separate mechanism — link to fortified guide. Note: fortified ≠ necessarily sweet (dry fino sherry).

2. **What Are the Main Styles of Dessert Wine?** — Cover 8 styles with 1-2 sentences each:
   - Sauternes (France/Bordeaux — noble rot, Semillon + Sauv Blanc, honey/apricot)
   - Tokaji (Hungary — noble rot, Furmint grapes, orange peel/apricot/honey)
   - Ice Wine / Eiswein (Canada/Germany — frozen grapes, intense sweetness, Riesling or Vidal)
   - Vin Santo (Italy/Tuscany — passito/straw dried, Trebbiano + Malvasia, nutty/fig)
   - Moscato d'Asti (Italy/Piedmont — low alcohol sparkling, peach/apricot/floral)
   - Late Harvest Riesling (Germany/Alsace — off-dry to sweet, high acidity, stone fruit)
   - PX Sherry (Spain — ultra-sweet, raisin/chocolate/fig, pour over ice cream)
   - Recioto della Valpolicella (Italy — dried Corvina grapes, sweet red, cherry/chocolate)

3. **What's the Difference Between Dessert Wine and Fortified Wine?** — Clarify: fortified wine has spirit added; not all fortified wines are sweet (dry fino, dry Madeira). Dessert wine = broader category covering sweetness regardless of method. PX sherry and Tawny Port appear in both lists. Link to fortified guide.

4. **How Sweet Is Dessert Wine?** — Residual sugar scale. Light (Moscato d'Asti: ~120 g/L) vs. medium (late harvest Riesling: 80-150 g/L) vs. rich (Sauternes: 120-200 g/L) vs. ultra-sweet (PX: 400+ g/L). Use this to pair.

5. **What Temperature Should You Serve Dessert Wine?** — Serving temperature by style + glassware note. Cover the "smaller glass" rule and why.

6. **What Food Should You Pair with Dessert Wine?** — Core rule: wine should be at least as sweet as the dessert. Cover 4-5 specific pairings. Address the chocolate trap: why dry red + dark chocolate is a common mistake (both have tannins that clash — PX or Recioto are better).

7. **FAQ** — 4-5 questions (PAA-style)

### Closing CTA
Natural nudge to the fortified wine guide or best sweet wines listicle.
