## Persona framing

Source: Miko's `audience.md` (2026-04-19).

**Three distinct buyers, one dominant hook:**

The primary reader is the **gift-giver under pressure**: someone heading to Christmas dinner at someone else's house who needs a bottle that won't embarrass them. They don't know the host's menu, they can't guess what the other guests drink, and their core anxiety is appearing cheap or clueless. Secondary reader is the **multi-palate host** buying across styles to cover the table (red for the beef crowd, white for the fish crowd, sparkling for the toast). Third is the **everyday drinker stepping up** for the occasion without venturing so far into unknown territory that they risk wasting money.

**The dominant hook: multi-palate anxiety.** Reddit evidence is unambiguous. The most-cited buying strategy is hedging across styles because "everyone should be happy with something." The article's structural advantage ŌĆö occasion-filtered tabs ŌĆö directly solves this problem. Lia should acknowledge this tension in the intro (Christmas tables have incompatible drinkers) and then frame each style bucket as the answer to a specific palate type.

**Top objections (Lia must address these, not dodge them):**
1. "I don't know what everyone drinks" ŌĆö solved by the occasion filter and cross-style coverage
2. "Good wine is expensive, I can't impress anyone" ŌĆö countered with specific value picks (Chateau Bourdieu 97pt/$19.99, E. Guigal 92pt/$17.97, CVNE Rioja 92pt/$19.97)
3. "Bold reds will clash with the food" ŌĆö redirected to medium-weight, high-acid reds (Lemelson Pinot Noir, CVNE Rioja, E. Guigal Rh├┤ne) as the smarter Christmas choice
4. "Ros├® isn't festive" ŌĆö tackled directly; Provence ros├® is the bridge wine for ham and charcuterie

**Purchase trigger:** Gifting deadline pressure. This is a 1ŌĆō2 week pre-Christmas decision window triggered by a dinner invite. Buyers default to "what I know" or "what was recommended on Reddit." The article wins by surfacing specific named bottles with a gift context and a clear price signal.

**Price sweet spot:** $25ŌĆō$40 gift confidence zone. Under $20 risks looking cheap. Over $50 risks showing off. The lineup covers value ($16.97 Matsu, $17.97 Guigal) through gifting sweet spot ($25ŌĆō$40 La Rioja Alta, Graham's Six Grapes).

**Tone guidance for Lia:**
- Speak directly to the gift-giver first, host second. "You're bringing a bottle" not "hosts may wish to consider."
- Use buyer-voice phrases from Miko's language bank (verbatim or adapted): "ol' reliable," "pairs with anything," "real crowd-pleaser," "punches above its weight," "drinks well right away," "easy win," "everyone should be happy with something."
- Do not open with wine taxonomy. Open with the social situation: the Christmas table with incompatible drinkers.
- High-acid mid-weight reds are the practical Christmas answer. Frame them as the smart choice, not the compromise choice.
- Beaujolais is the crowd-pleaser anchor mentioned most on Reddit. It's not in Lena's verified set (no verified Wine.com listing in this session's research), but Lia can reference the style by name in the Rh├┤ne/Pinot descriptions as a comparison anchor. Do not build an H3 around it unless Lena adds a verified listing.
