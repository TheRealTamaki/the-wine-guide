## Search Intent

Gift-givers choosing a bottle (or bottles) for Mum. The buyer is: adult children (sons and daughters), husbands and partners, family groups. Budget spans from "a thoughtful thank-you" ($15–25) through "proper treat" ($30–60) to one or two premium splurge picks ($60+). The search is a gift task, not a personal-drinking decision — the buyer is thinking about Mum's palate, not their own.

Sub-intents:
- "What style does Mum actually like?" (whites, rosé, sparkling, lighter reds)
- "What looks like a gift?" (presentation matters more than for personal bottles)
- "What pairs with Mother's Day brunch or lunch?"
- "Is wine even a good gift, or should I add something to it?"

---

## Angle variants considered

**Variant A: "The gift-giver's guide"** — framed around buying for someone else. Every wine chosen for Mum's palate, not the buyer's. Strongest match to intent. **CHOSEN.**

**Variant B: "By style: sparkling, white, rosé, red"** — clean navigation, useful for skimmers. But risks feeling generic (Valentine's and Christmas already do style-splits). Less differentiated.

**Variant C: "By budget tier"** — good for gift shoppers who are price-led. But budget-first framing makes the guide feel transactional, not thoughtful. Undermines the Mother's Day emotional context.

**Why Variant A:** The hook should put the reader in the moment of picking for someone they know. That's specific, emotional, and distinct from the other occasion guides (Wedding = crowd-scale; Housewarming = the gift that doesn't need a speech; Valentine's = she likes wine but which one; Christmas = cover everyone at the table). Mother's Day is the one where you're buying for one person you love and you want to get it exactly right.
