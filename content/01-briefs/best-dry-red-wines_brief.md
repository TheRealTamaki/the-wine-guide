## Search Intent

Reader either (a) saw "dry red wine" in a recipe and wants to know which bottle to actually buy, or (b) has tasted enough wine to know they don't like sweet reds and wants a shortlist of confidently dry options. They want clarity on what "dry" actually means (no residual sugar, not "tastes harsh") and a tight list of bottles that fit. Many are cooking-driven, so practical food and cooking notes earn the click.

## Wine Lineup Plan

**8 dry reds**, mix of structure levels and price bands $14–$30. The list should cover the classic dry red profile (firm tannin, low to no residual sugar, savoury fruit) across the styles a curious drinker actually encounters.

- **Cabernet Sauvignon** (the textbook dry red, full structure)
- **Bordeaux blend / Cabernet-Merlot** (the classic French dry red)
- **Tempranillo / Rioja** (Spanish dry red, food-friendly)
- **Sangiovese / Chianti Classico** (Italian dry red, savoury cherry)
- **Syrah / Shiraz** (peppery, dark dry red)
- **Malbec** (smoother dry red, popular gateway)
- **Pinot Noir** (lighter dry red, lower tannin but still bone-dry)
- **Cabernet Franc** (herbal, structured, slightly under-the-radar)

**Selection criteria:**
- All under $30
- Critic score 88+ where listed, customer rating 4.0+ where available
- Genuinely dry (residual sugar under 4 g/L; no off-dry styles like Lambrusco or sweet red blends)
- Mix of accessible producers and one or two slightly less obvious picks
