## Angle

Seafood is less about one magic white wine and more about weight, richness, and sauce. Raw shellfish wants very crisp wine. Delicate white fish wants light whites. Lobster and crab can handle Chardonnay. Salmon and tuna can take rose or Pinot Noir. Spicy shrimp and richer sauces need acidity, fruit, or a little sweetness.

## Core Pairing Guidance

- Oysters, clams, mussels: Muscadet, Chablis, Champagne, Cava, Albarino.
- Lean white fish: Pinot Grigio, Vermentino, Sauvignon Blanc, Gruner Veltliner, unoaked Chardonnay.
- Lobster and crab: Chardonnay, white Burgundy, Champagne, Chenin Blanc.
- Scallops: Chablis, Champagne, white Burgundy, Vermentino.
- Shrimp and prawns: Albarino, Sauvignon Blanc, Vermentino, rose, Riesling for spice.
- Salmon and tuna: Pinot Noir, dry rose, Chardonnay, Gamay, Grenache.
- Oily fish like mackerel and sardines: Albarino, dry rose, sparkling wine, Txakoli, Vermentino.
- Fried seafood: sparkling wine, Champagne, Cava, dry Prosecco, high-acid white.
- Spicy seafood: off-dry Riesling, Gewurztraminer, sparkling rose, lower-alcohol rose.
