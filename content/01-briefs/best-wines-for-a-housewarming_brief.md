## Search intent

The searcher is almost always the **gift-giver**, not the homeowner. They're going to a housewarming party in a day or two and want to bring something that:
- Looks presentable sitting on a counter when guests arrive
- Is recognisable enough to feel like a real choice (not grab-the-cheapest)
- Doesn't require a wine speech to explain
- Pairs with the food a housewarming typically serves: charcuterie, pizza, takeout, appetisers

Sweet spot: **$20-$50**, with 1-2 premium options for the "impressing the in-laws" use case.
Budget entry: at least one sub-$20 option for when you're one of eight guests bringing a bottle.

## Angle variants considered

1. **The "I don't know what they drink" angle** — acknowledge the gift-giver's core problem: you don't know the homeowner's taste. Frame picks as crowd-pleasers by style, giving the reader permission to choose with confidence.
2. **The "every housewarming food scenario" angle** — structure by what's typically served (charcuterie, pizza night, proper dinner party). Practical, food-forward.
3. **The "price bracket" angle** — section by spend: under $25, $25-$50, splurge. Clean for gift-givers with a set budget.

**Chosen angle:** Variant 1 (the "I don't know what they drink" framing), but structured loosely by style (sparkling, white, red, rosé) so the gift-giver can match to what they know the homeowner gravitates toward. This mirrors how top-ranking pages structure it and gives the most NW term absorption surface.

## Picks target

**13-15 wines.** Top competitor lists 15+ wines. SERP median is ~12. Split across:
- 3 sparkling/Champagne/Prosecco

- 4-5 red (Pinot Noir, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot/blend, one bold option)
- 2 rosé (Provence, one value option)
- 1-2 premium/splurge options
