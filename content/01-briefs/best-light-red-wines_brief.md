## Search Intent

Reader is looking for red wines that aren't heavy, tannic, or "wine snob serious." They want something they can drink chilled, pair with chicken and salmon, sip on a hot afternoon, or share with friends who don't normally like red. Often this is the audience that finds Cab Sauv too aggressive and big Shiraz too punishing.

## Wine Lineup Plan

**8 light reds**, mostly under $25, balanced across regions:
- **Pinot Noir** (different producer than dry-reds page)
- **Beaujolais / Gamay** (the textbook light red)
- **Frappato** (Sicilian)
- **Mencia / Bierzo** (Spanish, light-medium)
- **Cinsault / Pinotage blend** (South African or French)
- **Schiava / Trentino**
- **Lambrusco Secco** (dry sparkling-style)
- **Trousseau, Poulsard, or Zweigelt** (lighter wildcard)

**Selection criteria:**
- Mostly under $25
- Light or light-medium body
- Lower tannin (under 5/10 perceived)
- Drink-now style (no need to age)
- Critic 88+ or 4.0+ buyer rating
