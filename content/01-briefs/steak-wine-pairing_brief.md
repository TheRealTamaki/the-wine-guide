## Angle

Steak pairing is about fat, char, seasoning, and sauce. Cabernet Sauvignon is the default for fatty grilled cuts, but softer reds work better with lean steak, herb sauces, and casual weeknight preparations. The article should make the reader feel confident at the bottle shop and at the table.

## Core Pairing Guidance

- Ribeye, New York strip, porterhouse: Cabernet Sauvignon, Bordeaux blend, Napa Cabernet, Coonawarra Cabernet.
- Filet mignon: Merlot, Cabernet Franc, right-bank Bordeaux, Pinot Noir if served with mushrooms.
- Sirloin, flank, skirt: Malbec, Tempranillo, Zinfandel, Cotes du Rhone.
- Prime rib: Cabernet Sauvignon, Bordeaux, Syrah, Rioja Gran Reserva.
- Steak au poivre: Syrah, Cabernet Franc, Rioja.
- Chimichurri: Malbec, Carmenere, Cabernet Franc.
- Blue cheese butter: Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, Port for a small finishing pour.
- Steak tacos or fajitas: Malbec, Zinfandel, Grenache, chilled lighter reds.
- Steak salad: Cabernet Franc, Cru Beaujolais, dry rose.

## Voice Notes

Keep the article practical, opinionated, and conversational. Avoid em-dashes and banned contrast patterns. Use specific cuts, sauces, and bottle styles. Explain tannin through what the reader feels in their mouth. Do not overcomplicate the reader's choice.
