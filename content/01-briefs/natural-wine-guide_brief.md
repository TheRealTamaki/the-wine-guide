## Target Reader

Curious wine drinker who keeps hearing "natural wine" at wine bars and bottle shops and wants to understand what it means without feeling lectured. They've probably tried one, found it weird, and want to know if that's normal. They're not looking for a manifesto — they want a straight answer.

---

## Required Coverage

- **Definition:** low-intervention winemaking, organic/biodynamic farming, native yeasts, minimal/no added sulphites, no fining or filtering
- **Distinctions:** how natural, organic, and biodynamic overlap and where they differ
- **Taste profile:** why it tastes different (funky, cloudy, sometimes pétillant, bottle variation)
- **Regions/movement:** France (Loire, Beaujolais), Italy, Georgia, Australia, USA
- **Key figures:** Jules Chauvet, Marcel Lapierre (write naturally, not as a list)
- **How to identify:** RAW Wine fair, importer markings, look for cloudiness + funky labels
- **Food pairings:** what works with natural wine's lively acidity and low tannin
- **Who it's for/not for:** set expectations honestly
- **Common myths:** not always cloudy, not always funky, no official certification body
