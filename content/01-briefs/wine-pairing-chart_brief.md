## Search Intent

Readers want a fast, practical chart they can use while planning dinner, ordering in a restaurant, or standing in a bottle shop. They need confidence more than theory. The page should answer "what wine goes with this food?" quickly, then explain enough logic to make the chart useful beyond one meal.

## Angle

Build the page as a printable-feeling cheat sheet with short explanations under each category. The article should give immediate pairings first, then the reasoning behind the biggest decisions: acid with fat, low tannin with delicate food, sweetness with spice, and matching wine weight to food weight.

## Voice Notes

- Conversational, practical, specific.
- No em-dashes.

- Use dish examples instead of abstract rules.
