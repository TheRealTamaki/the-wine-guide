## Quick Answer
Most sushi wants high-acid white or sparkling wine. Champagne, dry Riesling, Albarino, Gruner Veltliner, and Chablis cover the table for sashimi, raw rolls, and lemony preparations. Salmon and tuna rolls open the door to dry rose or chilled Pinot Noir. Spicy rolls want off-dry Riesling. Skip big tannic reds.

## Hook angle
Sushi night, three different rolls hitting the table at once, someone reaching for the Cabernet "because it goes with everything." Pay off with what to pour instead.
