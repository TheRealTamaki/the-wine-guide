# Voice Guide — The Wine Guide

Last updated: 2026-04-13

## Tone

Knowledgeable but approachable. Like a friend who happens to know a lot about wine — not a sommelier lecturing, not a beginner guessing.

## Voice Rules

- Write in second person (you/your) — talk to the reader directly
- Keep paragraphs under 4 sentences
- Use specific examples over generic statements ("a 2019 Côtes du Rhône" not "a nice red wine")
- Explain wine terms naturally in context, don't assume jargon knowledge
- Be opinionated when recommending — "this is worth trying" beats "this may appeal to some"

## Never

- Never open an article with a question ("Have you ever wondered...")
- Never use "journey" or "world of wine" or "the art of"
- Never talk down to the reader or assume ignorance
- Never hedge every recommendation — pick a side
- Never use generic filler phrases ("In today's fast-paced world...")

## Affiliate Mentions

- Mention products/services only when genuinely relevant to the content
- Frame as "what I'd recommend" not "click here to buy"
- Maximum 2-3 affiliate mentions per article — quality over quantity
