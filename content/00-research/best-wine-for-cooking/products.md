## Lineup overview

The 15-wine set covers every major cooking use case: braising reds (Merlot, Pinot Noir), pan sauces and deglazing whites (Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Pinot Grigio), and the two fortified anchors the SERP will expect (Marsala for Chicken Marsala, dry Amontillado Sherry for soups and bisques). Price spread runs $15.97 (Florio Marsala, Attems Pinot Grigio entry point) to $32.97 (Migration Pinot Noir), with most wines sitting in the $18–$27 sweet spot — affordable enough to use in a recipe without guilt. Every wine carries either a 90+ critic score or a verified ≥4.0 customer rating. The lineup deliberately avoids generic cooking wines or "cooking wine" labelled products — all 15 are real drinking wines, supporting the core editorial message: cook with wine you'd actually drink.

## Candidates (15 wines — final count, no cut buffer)

### 1. Oberon Merlot 2023 — $25.97 — Dry Red (Merlot)
Oberon is California's most crowd-friendly Merlot at this price, with 91 JS and 91 WS critic scores and a standout 4.4 customer rating from 31 reviews — the highest customer rating among the reds in this lineup. Paso Robles fruit gives it soft tannins and ripe plum character that translates beautifully into braised short ribs, beef bourguignon, and red pasta sauces. At $25.97 it's the kind of bottle you'd happily pour a glass from while cooking. Top red pick: solid name recognition, strong dual critic + customer signal.

### 2. Schug Sonoma Coast Pinot Noir 2023 — $19.99 — Dry Red (Pinot Noir)
One of the best-value cooking Pinot Noirs on Wine.com right now — marked down from $32 to $19.99, 91 JS and 91 TP, 3.6 customer rating from 79 reviews (the most-reviewed Pinot Noir in the search). Sonoma Coast Pinot Noir is the classic choice for coq au vin, duck confit, and mushroom risotto: bright acidity, red fruit, moderate tannins. The sale price makes it a no-brainer "use in the recipe and pour a glass" scenario. Top 100 of 2025.

### 3. Lemelson Thea's Selection Pinot Noir 2022 — $29.99 — Dry Red (Pinot Noir)
Willamette Valley Pinot at the premium end of the cooking red budget. 93 Decanter, 91 WS, 91 Vinous — serious scores for a sub-$30 Oregon Pinot. 3.6 customer rating from 35 reviews. Lighter-bodied than the California examples, which makes it better suited to delicate dishes: wild mushroom sauces, duck breast, salmon with a red wine reduction. The earthy, foresty character reads as more "French" in the pan than the Schug.

### 4. Chehalem Mountains Pinot Noir 2022 — $24.99 — Dry Red (Pinot Noir)
93 WS, 91 JS, 90 JD — triple critic sign-off. 3.8 customer rating from 19 reviews. Another Willamette Valley Pinot at a notably lower price than its scores suggest; on sale from $50 to $24.99 (50% off). For cooking, the moderate acidity and red-cherry profile suits salmon dishes, pork tenderloin, and lighter braises. Adds style diversity to the red bucket alongside the Oberon and Schug.

### 5. Migration Sonoma Coast Pinot Noir 2022 — $32.97 — Dry Red (Pinot Noir)
The premium red in the lineup. 94 JD, 93 JS, 92 WW — strong three-critic stack. 3.8 customer rating from 8 reviews (fewer reviews but high). On sale from $46 to $32.97. Duckhorn's Migration label is one of the most recognised Sonoma Pinot brands at this tier, which helps the article's credibility. For cooking: complex enough that it rewards restraint — better suited as a recipe wine for a special-occasion dish than an everyday braising wine. Gives Lia a "step up" tier to write against.

### 6. Astrolabe Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc 2024 — $19.97 — Dry White (Sauvignon Blanc)
New Zealand SB is the default cooking white for seafood, clam sauce, and white fish. 93 JS, 91 RP, 90 Vinous, 90 WS — a four-critic pile-up at $19.97 is rare. 3.6 customer rating from 19 reviews. Marlborough's high-acid, grassy style cuts through butter and cream better than California SB, making it a genuine functional choice — not just "a nice white." Top cooking use case: sautéed shrimp, white clam sauce, lemon-butter pan sauces.

### 7. Schug Sauvignon Blanc 2023 — $19.97 — Dry White (Sauvignon Blanc)
92 TP, 90 JS, 90 WE — solid three-critic result from Sonoma. On sale from $30 to $19.97 (33% off). 3.8 customer rating from 29 reviews. California SB tends to be richer and more melon-forward than NZ, which suits risotto and cream sauces better than the Astrolabe. Pairing both SBs in the lineup (NZ and California) gives Lia a natural contrast to write — classic versus richer styles for different cooking applications.

### 8. Diatom Santa Barbara Chardonnay 2024 — $24.97 — Dry White (Chardonnay)
Top 100 of 2025. 94 JD, 93 WS, 93 JS, 92 WE, 90 RP — the most-decorated white in the lineup. 3.7 customer rating from 32 reviews. Santa Barbara Chardonnay at this tier is typically unoaked or lightly oaked, which makes it ideal for delicate cooking: steamed mussels, beurre blanc, chicken in white wine sauce. The freshness holds up in the pan better than a heavily buttered, oaky California style. Best-on-paper pick for white cooking.

### 9. Banshee Sonoma Coast Chardonnay 2022 — $17.97 — Dry White (Chardonnay)
93 JS, 90 W&S. On sale from $28 to $17.97 (36% off). 3.5 customer rating from 35 reviews. The value-play Chardonnay — almost as impressive on paper as the Diatom but at $7 less, making it the "everyday cooking Chardonnay" slot. Sonoma Coast style tends toward the leaner end, good acidity, stone fruit. Strong "cook with it and finish the bottle" appeal at this price.

### 10. Terlan Pinot Grigio 2024 — $26.97 — Dry White (Pinot Grigio)
93 JS, 92 WE, 91 RP, 90 Vinous — four critics, Alto Adige pedigree. 3.8 customer rating from 40 reviews. Terlaner Pinot Grigio is one of the benchmark names in the Alto Adige style: mineral, textured, dry, with good weight. That structure makes it better for cooking than thin, watery versions. Best used in: seafood risotto, pan-seared white fish, clams, cream sauces. The premium Pinot Grigio anchor.

### 11. Attems Pinot Grigio 2023 — $18.97 — Dry White (Pinot Grigio)
90 JS. On sale from $24 to $18.97. 3.8 customer rating from 15 reviews. Friuli-Venezia Giulia Pinot Grigio — the regional style most home cooks picture when a recipe calls for "a dry Italian white." Crisp, light, clean — good for deglazing, light pasta dishes, shellfish. Pairs with the Terlan as the value alternative in the same varietal bucket.

### 12. St. Michael-Eppan Pinot Grigio 2024 — $24.97 — Dry White (Pinot Grigio)
93 JS, 91 WW. 4.4 customer rating from 50 reviews — the highest customer rating in the white wine bucket. Alto Adige producer with strong brand recognition. The 4.4/50 signal is the strongest customer endorsement among the whites. For cooking: similar application to the Terlan, slightly richer and more rounded. Gives Lia three Pinot Grigio options across three price points ($18.97 / $24.97 / $26.97) — useful for the listicle.

### 13. Neyers Sonoma 304 Chardonnay 2023 — $22.97 — Dry White (Chardonnay)
92 WS, 90 JS. 4.1 customer rating from 13 reviews — clears the 4.0 customer bar. Slightly fuller body than the Banshee or Diatom, suits richer cream-based cooking: pasta primavera, chicken piccata, carbonara-style sauces. On sale from $25 to $22.97. Fills the "mid-weight cooking Chardonnay" slot between the leaner Banshee and the lighter Diatom.

### 14. Florio Vecchioflorio Sweet Marsala Superiore 2020 — $15.97 — Fortified (Marsala)
90 WE. 4.7 customer rating from 28 reviews — the highest customer rating in the entire lineup. Classic Sicilian Marsala from the most-recognisable Marsala brand. This is the bottle people search for when they type "best wine for cooking chicken marsala." Sweet Marsala Superiore is the style most recipes call for. At $15.97 it sits at the accessible entry of the price band. Essential fortified anchor — no "best wine for cooking" article is complete without Marsala.

### 15. Lustau Solera Los Arcos Dry Amontillado Sherry — $19.97 — Fortified (Dry Sherry)
93 RP, 92 JS, 91 Decanter, 90 WS — four strong critics. 3.6 customer rating from 186 reviews — by far the most-reviewed product in the lineup (186 reviews signals strong purchase volume, not a niche bottle). Amontillado is the driest widely-available Sherry style and the classic choice for: French onion soup, bisques, mushroom sauces, pan sauces for pork. The nutty, savoury flavour is irreplaceable in recipes that call for dry sherry. Lustau is Spain's most credible Sherry export brand at this price. Essential second fortified anchor.
