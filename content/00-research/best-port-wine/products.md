## Lineup overview

Port is a style-segmented category: Ruby, Tawny (NV, 10yr, 20yr), LBV, Vintage, and White Port each appeal to a different buyer. The SERP for "best port wine" typically organises by style bucket — our lineup mirrors that. Ruby and basic Tawny sit firmly in the $15–$40 band. Aged Tawnies (20yr) and the Colheita run $49–$62, which is appropriate and expected: any "best port" list that omits a 20-year Tawny is missing its most gifted bottle. Both 20yr Tawnies are flagged as price-ceiling stretches in the JSON. White Port is stocked ($19.97 each) — two strong options from Fonseca and Niepoort. No affordable Vintage Port made the cut (cheapest vintage on Wine.com is $80+), so the LBV (Fonseca 2016, $23.97) fills the Vintage-adjacent slot. Zuccardi Malamado (Malbec Port, Argentina, $24.97) is included as an "other" option if Jas wants a non-Portuguese angle. 15 candidates delivered.
