## Lineup overview

Sangria is a value-intent keyword — the bottle goes in the pitcher, so spending over $25 is a hard sell. This lineup holds to $15–$25 with one noted stretch ($22.97 Whispering Angel, justified by its rosé-sangria name recognition on every SERP). The style mix covers the full sangria landscape: six Spanish reds (three Garnacha, two Rioja Tempranillo, one Ribera del Duero Tempranillo) as the SERP core, two Malbecs plus Catena as the Argentina contingent that consistently appears in sangria listicles, two Provencal rosés plus one Languedoc rosé for rosé-sangria variants, and two whites (Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc and Friulian Pinot Grigio) for white sangria. Every candidate is confirmed live on Wine.com with real Cloudinary image IDs. Critic scores are recorded exactly as shown on the product page — no inflation.

## Candidates (over-delivered — 15 to let Lia cut to SERP median)

### 1. Matsu El Picaro Tempranillo 2024 — $16.97 — Red, Spanish Tempranillo
Toro Tempranillo at $16.97 with 91 pts Wilfred Wong and a verified 4.7★ from 40 customer reviews — the strongest customer-rating signal in the entire red pool. Toro Tempranillo is rounder and plummier than Rioja, which makes it one of the most forgiving sangria bases: the fruit survives the dilution. Price slot anchor at the low end of the band. Has appeared in mulled-wine and cheap-red lineups already on this site, so Lia knows the voice angle.

### 2. CVNE Rioja Reserva 2020 — $19.97 — Red, Rioja Tempranillo
Five-critic consensus wine: 92 James Suckling, 92 Vinous, 91 Robert Parker, 91 Wine Spectator, 90 Decanter. On sale from $29 — strong perceived value angle for the article. Rioja Reserva is the textbook sangria recommendation on every editorial SERP (VinePair, Kitchn, Epicurious all lead with Rioja). Garnet, cherry-forward, moderate tannin. The "proper Spanish sangria" pick that anchors the lineup's credibility.

### 3. Bardos Vinedos de Altura Tempranillo 2022 — $19.97 — Red, Ribera del Duero
90 James Suckling, 90 Wilfred Wong, verified 4.5★ from 52 reviews — second-strongest customer-rating signal in the red pool. Ribera del Duero Tempranillo runs bolder than Rioja with more dark fruit and structure, which holds up well in a fruit-forward sangria. On sale from $23. Covers the "bold red sangria" style slot distinct from the two Rioja picks.

### 4. Torre de Ona by La Rioja Alta Finca San Martin Crianza 2021 — $19.97 — Red, Rioja Tempranillo
92 Robert Parker, 91 James Suckling, 91 Vinous, 91 Wine Spectator — four-critic stack at the $19.97 price point. La Rioja Alta is one of the most respected Rioja houses; Torre de Ona is the accessible entry. Crianza designation means oak-aged with cherry, vanilla, and gentle spice — a classic sangria profile. Second Rioja slot gives Lia a choice between the CVNE (more fruit-forward) and this one (more oak structure).

### 5. Bodegas Breca El Nacido Garnacha 2023 — $17.97 — Red, Spanish Garnacha
91 James Suckling, 90 Robert Parker, 89 Wine Spectator, 3.9★ from 20 reviews. Calatayud Garnacha from one of Spain's most decorated Garnacha producers at $17.97 (sale from $20). Garnacha is the original sangria grape — high berry fruit, lower tannin, natural sweetness that pairs with citrus and brandy. The Breca El Nacido is the most score-laden Garnacha in the lineup at this price.

### 6. Borsao Tres Picos Garnacha 2021 — $17.97 — Red, Spanish Garnacha
91 James Suckling, 3.6★ from 30 reviews. Campo de Borja Garnacha, consistently one of Wine Spectator's Best Value picks across multiple vintages (90 WS and 90 WE on the 2020). The Tres Picos name is recognisable to wine-curious readers who've seen it on restaurant lists. At $17.97, it's the go-to "reliable, affordable Spanish red" slot — Lia can frame it as the straightforward sangria workhorse.

### 7. Bodegas Nekeas El Chaparral de Vega Sindoa Old Vines Garnacha 2022 — $18.97 — Red, Spanish Garnacha
92 James Suckling, 3.9★ from 11 reviews. Navarra Garnacha described as "the most Atlantic Garnacha in Spain" — higher acidity and red-fruit brightness versus the riper Campo de Borja style. Old-vine concentration at $18.97. Third Garnacha slot gives Lia style diversity across the three Garnacha picks (Calatayud / Campo de Borja / Navarra). Lia can cut to one or two Garnachas; all three are here for flexibility.

### 8. BenMarco Malbec 2022 — $19.99 — Red, Argentine Malbec
93 James Suckling, 91 Vinous, 90 Wine Spectator, verified 4.2★ from 104 reviews — the largest customer-review pool in the entire lineup. On sale from $25 (20% off). Uco Valley Malbec from Susana Balbo's négociant label, which has already shipped in the best-cheap-red-wine article. Malbec is SERP-standard for sangria (dark cherry, plum, soft tannin, good fruit extraction); BenMarco delivers it at a price that passes the sangria-value test. The 104-review count is a trust signal Lia can cite.

### 9. Tapiz Alta Collection Malbec 2022 — $18.97 — Red, Argentine Malbec
93 Vinous, 90 Wine Enthusiast, on sale from $20. Uco Valley Malbec at the value end of the Malbec slot. Slightly lower profile than BenMarco but strong critic scores and a lower price point. Ships Wednesday (vs Monday for most others) — note for Lia if fulfillment timing matters for any copy angle.

### 10. Catena Malbec 2024 — $19.97 — Red, Argentine Malbec
No critic scores yet for the 2024 vintage (new release), but Catena is one of the most recognised Malbec brands globally and the brand name alone carries reader trust. The 2023 scored 90 RP; the 2022 scored 92 Vinous and 91 RP. Lia should pass `score: 0` and `critic: ""` in the component and lead with brand recognition and prior-vintage pattern in the description. Third Malbec slot — Lia will likely cut to one or two; Catena is the name-recognition pick, BenMarco is the score pick.

### 11. Miraval Rosé 2024 — $19.97 — Rosé, Côtes de Provence
92 Decanter, 92 James Suckling, 90 Robert Parker, 4.0★ from 19 reviews. On sale from $25. Côtes de Provence Provencal rosé — the rosé category's most recognised sangria recommendation on editorial SERPs. Brad Pitt / Angelina Jolie provenance adds name-recognition hook. Pale Provencal style: strawberry, citrus, mineral — bright and clean in a rosé sangria pitcher. Primary rosé pick.

### 12. Chateau d'Esclans Whispering Angel Rosé 2024 — $22.97 — Rosé, Côtes de Provence
92 James Suckling, 3.9★ from 41 reviews. Largest customer-review pool of the three rosés. At $22.97 (sale from $26) this sits at the upper edge of the band — noted stretch but justified: Whispering Angel is the single most searched rosé brand in the US and it appears in virtually every best-rosé-for-sangria search result. Lia should note the higher price vs the Miraval and frame it as the premium rosé-sangria pick.

### 13. Hampton Water Rosé 2024 — $19.97 — Rosé, Languedoc
3.8★ from 20 reviews; no critic scores for the 2024 vintage (2023 scored 91 Decanter, 2022 scored 90 Decanter and 90 Wilfred Wong). Languedoc rather than Provence — lighter on prestige but lower price. Jon Bon Jovi co-ownership adds a pop-culture hook. Weakest score signal of the three rosés; Lia will likely cut this one if she needs to trim to two rosés. Included for the band-friendly price and the celebrity angle.

### 14. Astrolabe Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc 2024 — $19.97 — White, Sauvignon Blanc
93 James Suckling, 91 Robert Parker, 90 Vinous, 90 Wine Spectator, 3.6★ from 19 reviews. Marlborough NZ Sauvignon Blanc is the SERP-standard white sangria grape — gooseberry, citrus, herbaceous zip that cuts through the sweetness of fruit and triple sec. Astrolabe is a respected Marlborough producer. Four-critic consensus at $19.97 makes this the strongest white option in the lineup. Primary white pick.

### 15. Attems Pinot Grigio 2023 — $18.97 — White, Pinot Grigio
90 James Suckling, 3.8★ from 15 reviews. On sale from $24. Friuli-Venezia Giulia Pinot Grigio — the other SERP-standard white sangria grape. Softer and rounder than the Sauvignon Blanc; better fit for readers who want a milder white sangria. Covers a distinct style slot from the Astrolabe. Lia can include both whites (one for zesty white sangria, one for mellow white sangria) or cut to one if the page needs to stay leaner.
