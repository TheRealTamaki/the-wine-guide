## Lineup overview

Valentine's Day is a gifting and romance keyword, not a pure value keyword. The lineup covers the four style pillars this occasion demands: sparkling (bubbles signal celebration), rosé (the most romantic wine colour, and Provence rosé dominates Valentine's editorial coverage), elegant light-to-medium reds (Pinot Noir is the SERP's most-recommended Valentine's red), and a dessert/Port finish. Price band is $15–$40 standard, with the Laurent-Perrier half-bottle at $34.99 and Iron Horse Wedding Cuvee at $39.99 sitting at the top end — both justify their position because gifting intent tolerates a stretch, and both have strong romance/occasion signals. No wine exceeds $40. Sparkling rosé is covered twice (Hampton Water Bubbly Rose and La Marca Prosecco Rosé) because sparkling rosé is a Valentine's SERP fixture. Port covers the chocolate-pairing angle that editorial competitors consistently include. Lia should set final count from Lino's SERP median and cut from this pool.

## Candidates (over-delivered — 15 to let Lia cut)

### SPARKLING

### 1. La Marca Prosecco NV — $18.97 — Prosecco / Brut
Wine.com's most popular Prosecco by volume. At $18.97 it anchors the value sparkling slot — accessible enough for a weeknight Valentine's dinner, giftable enough to feel intentional. 4.7-star customer rating (1,842 reviews) from best-prosecco research. Glera, Veneto. Pairings: aperitif, strawberries, light cheese board. The go-to answer for readers who want bubbles without spending Champagne money.

### 2. La Marca Prosecco Rosé 2023 — $18.97 — Sparkling Rosé / Brut
The pink sibling at the same price. Covers the sparkling rosé slot that dominates Valentine's listicles. Peach, rose petal, red fruit aromatics. 4.3-star rating from previous research. Veneto. Pairings: strawberries, light desserts, charcuterie. Strong occasion signal — the bottle is visually pink and reads as Valentine's at a glance.

### 3. Laurent-Perrier La Cuvee Brut 375ML NV — $34.99 — Champagne / Brut
Half-bottle format is the single most giftable Champagne format for two people on Valentine's Day. 95 Tasting Panel, 94 James Suckling, 91 Wine Enthusiast, 91 Wine Spectator, 90 Jeb Dunnuck, 90 Decanter, 90 Robert Parker. Customer rating 4.2/164 reviews. Champagne, France (NV). Pairings: oysters, brie, strawberries. The half-bottle angle is a strong differentiator — no competing Valentine's editorial page I've seen uses this format specifically, and it's genuinely the right size for two.

### 4. Iron Horse Wedding Cuvee 2019 — $39.99 — California Sparkling / Brut
"Wedding Cuvee" is the most romantically named bottle on Wine.com. 93 Wine Spectator, 93 James Suckling. 3.9/13 reviews. Green Valley, Russian River, Sonoma County, California. Pairings: shellfish, roasted chicken, celebration toasts. At $39.99 (sale from $58) it's the splurge sparkling slot with a name that practically writes its own Valentine's headline. Iron Horse is also one of the most awarded California sparkling producers.

### 5. Hampton Water Bubbly Rose NV — $29.99 — Sparkling Rosé
Jon Bon Jovi's Languedoc sparkling rosé. 91 Wilfred Wong, 4.0/10 reviews. South of France. Celebrity angle is a natural gifting hook. Light pink, red-fruit forward, festive packaging. Pairings: strawberries, charcuterie, light seafood. Sits neatly in the mid-price sparkling slot between La Marca ($18.97) and Laurent-Perrier ($34.99).
