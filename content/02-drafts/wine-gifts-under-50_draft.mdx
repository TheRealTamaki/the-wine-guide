## Pair Each Gift With the Right Wine

A wine gift becomes a perfect gift when you pair it with a bottle that fits. A wine lover or enthusiast notices the thought, and the wine gift set lands as a curated gift instead of a generic accessory drop.

For the Le Chateau Crystal Decanter, pair with a young Cabernet Sauvignon, a Merlot from Napa Valley, or a fine wine from a vineyard you've actually visited. The wide bowl aerates the bottle of wine fast, which suits high-quality 750ml bottles that have been sitting in the cellar.

For the Vinturi aerator and the Vinvoli 3-Stage, pair with bold reds that benefit from oxygen: a Cabernet, a Piedmont Barolo, or a vintage Bordeaux. Add a stocking stuffers pack of food gifts (truffle salt, aged cheese) for a beautifully packaged combination that punches well above $50.

For the YETI Rambler and the Homeries Marble Chiller, pair with a crisp white wine. A Chardonnay, a Sauvignon Blanc from Sancerre, a sparkling wine for celebrations, or a Brut Champagne all stay cold long enough to drink slowly. A Moscato or a rosé works for a sweeter angle.

For the Spiegelau red wine glasses, pair with a Pinot Noir, a Cabernet, or a holiday red blend. The set of 4 turns into the holiday wine gift that anchors a dinner party. A 375ml half-bottle fits the gift box neatly when the recipient lives alone.

For the Moocoo Electric Opener and the Vintorio Professional Corkscrew, pair with two wine bottles in different styles: a vintage Cabernet for the cellar and an inexpensive Tuesday-night red. A limited edition bottle from a winemaker the recipient already follows turns the gift into one of the best gifts for wine drinkers on your list.

If the recipient prefers a basket-style present, a small wine basket or gift baskets pack with two 375ml bottles, a corkscrew, and food gifts (chocolate, charcuterie) covers the bases. Wine gift cards work as a backup when you're shopping last-minute.
