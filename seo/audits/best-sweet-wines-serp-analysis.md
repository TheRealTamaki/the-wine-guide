## 6. Novel feature recommendations (no competitor has these)

### Novel #1 (PRIMARY — recommended ship): **Sweetness Intensity Scale per wine**

Every competitor talks about sweetness tiers in prose (off-dry → semi-sweet → sweet → dessert). **Nobody shows it as a visual indicator per bottle.** Ship a per-wine 5-dot sweetness meter rendered inline with the AffiliateCTA or in a new `<WineDataBlock>`:

- **1 dot:** Off-dry (e.g. Riesling Kabinett, German Halbtrocken)
- **2 dots:** Semi-sweet (e.g. Moscato d'Asti, Brachetto, White Zinfandel)
- **3 dots:** Sweet (e.g. Late Harvest Riesling, Ruby Port, Lambrusco Dolce)
- **4 dots:** Rich/dessert-sweet (e.g. Sauternes, Tokaji Aszú 5 Puttonyos, Vintage Port)
- **5 dots:** Ultra-sweet (e.g. Tokaji Aszú 6 Puttonyos, Tokaji Eszencia, Ice Wine, Pedro Ximénez)

Why it wins: the single biggest pain point surfaced in the 8 Reddit-style PAAs is "I don't know how sweet is too sweet." A visual scale solves that instantly. Reuses a dot-meter primitive trivially shareable with other listicles.

### Novel #2: **Sweetness × Body 2D positioning matrix**

Same primitive as the `good-cheap-wine` recommendation, applied to the sweet-wine spectrum:
- **X-axis:** Sweetness 1–5 (off-dry → ultra-sweet)
- **Y-axis:** Body 1–5 (light & fizzy → full & fortified)
- All 12–15 bottles plotted as labelled dots. Clusters will emerge naturally: Moscato d'Asti / Brachetto in the light-sweet quadrant, Port / Sauternes / Tokaji in the full-rich quadrant, Late Harvest Riesling in the medium zone.

Zero competitors have any 2D visualisation. Single biggest visual differentiator. Build the `<DotScaleRow>` primitive once, reuse on `good-cheap-wine` and future pages (`best-red-wine-under-20` audit queued this feature at item #6 too — shared build).

### Novel #3: **"How is this wine made sweet?" micro-tag per bottle**

Each wine card carries a one-word production-method tag:
- **Late Harvest** (e.g. Late Harvest Riesling)
- **Noble Rot / Botrytis** (e.g. Sauternes, Tokaji Aszú, Auslese)
- **Fortified** (e.g. Port, Madeira, PX Sherry)
- **Ice Wine / Eiswein** (e.g. Inniskillin Ice Wine, Eiswein)
- **Stopped Fermentation** (e.g. Moscato d'Asti, Asti Spumante, Brachetto)
- **Varietal / Naturally Sweet** (e.g. Lambrusco Dolce, White Zinfandel — fermentation stopped with residual varietal sugar)

No competitor labels production method per bottle. Educational + E-E-A-T + NW entity absorption (noble rot, botrytis, fortified, late harvest, eiswein all become naturally-placed entity terms).

### Novel #4: **Sweetness sub-navigator chip strip above the wine listings**

Top of the wine list, before H2 #1, a row of anchor chips:
`[Off-Dry] [Semi-Sweet] [Sweet Reds] [Fortified & Dessert] [Ice Wine]`

Each chip scrolls to the relevant H2 bucket. Fastest-to-value UX for readers who already know they want a specific style. Cheap to ship if we adopt the same primitive as `good-cheap-wine`'s price-tier chip strip.

**Recommended ship order:** Novel #1 (Sweetness dots) + Novel #3 (production tag) are both trivial prop additions to existing components — ship both. Novel #2 (2D matrix) is the signature visual, share primitive with `good-cheap-wine` and `best-red-wine-under-20`. Novel #4 (chip strip) if the shared primitive from `good-cheap-wine` has already landed when Lia opens the brief.
