## Gap analysis — what top pages have that we don't

1. **Shop-owner / expert curation framing.** Serious Eats uses 4 named wine-shop owners as a framing device — each gets a photo, bio, and 2–3 recommendations. This is the single strongest E-E-A-T move on the SERP and we have no equivalent. We rely on Claire Bennett's byline but don't surface an expert-panel format.
2. **"Labels to look for" per varietal.** Cellar Notes' Substack gives a specific list of appellation/label keywords for each grape ("Vinho Verde, Rias Baixas" for Albariño; "Muscadet, Muscadet Sèvre et Maine" for Melon de Bourgogne). We mention appellations inside prose but don't surface them as a scannable "look for these words on the label" callout. This is functionally a shopping checklist and reads like ground truth.
3. **Style-tier framing at article level.** Suhru Vineyards splits its guide into "Crisp & Refreshing / Balanced & Textured / Rich & Creamy" as top-level H2s. Since our page is specifically about *crisp* whites, we could invert this: frame our 8 wines as "Ultra-crisp / Crisp with texture / Crisp with aromatic edge" sub-groupings. Our WineQuiz already uses these 4 lanes (crisp-fruity / aromatic / mineral / bone-dry) — we just don't reflect them in the page's H2 hierarchy.
4. **"When to drink them" pairing-by-occasion blocks.** Suhru's guide ends each tier with "best for: salads / aperitifs / fish" etc. We cover this per wine but not at the style-tier level.
5. **Exclusion-set hook.** Lidey Likes and Cellar Notes both explicitly frame their lists as "not Sauv Blanc or Pinot Grigio" — i.e. deliberately avoiding the obvious picks. Our article includes Santa Margherita PG and both NZ Sauv Blancs, which is editorially correct for the keyword but means we miss the "I already know PG/SB, show me something else" reader segment. That reader exists; we don't serve them structurally.
6. **Specific ABV + residual sugar data per wine.** We already include ABV per wine — this is a strength. Residual sugar data we imply via "dry / off-dry" labels but don't surface as a number. None of the competitors have this either (noted under novel opportunity).

**What we have that they don't (for preservation):**
- WineQuiz routing readers by food + occasion + style preference. Nobody on the SERP has an interactive quiz.
- GERD-friendly FAQ question (for acid-reflux readers). Genuinely unique on the SERP.
- Extended FAQ with 9 additional varietal callouts (Riesling / Chenin / Sancerre / Assyrtiko / Pinot Gris / Aligoté / Bordeaux Blanc / Viognier / Albariño) — this is the most thorough "other varietals worth trying" coverage on the SERP.
- 7-region coverage in 8 wines (ratio tighter than Liquor.com's 11 wines across similar regional spread).
