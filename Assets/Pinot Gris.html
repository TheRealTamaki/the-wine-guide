Vitis vinifera · White grape

Pinot Gris

/ˈpiːnoʊ ˈɡriː/   “pee-no gree”

A food-friendly white wine made from a grayish-pink grape. Depending on where it’s grown and who makes it, it can be crisp and lemony (Italian Pinot Grigio) or rich, honeyed, and aromatic (Alsatian Pinot Gris) — same grape, two personalities.

The grape itself

A mutation of Pinot Noir — same DNA, different skin color.

Pinot Gris berries aren’t green like most white grapes — they’re dusty pink, grayish, or even bluish (hence gris, French for “gray”). That extra skin pigment is why some Pinot Gris wines have a gentle copper or salmon tint.

  1. Growing the grapes

    Vines are pruned in winter and tended all summer. Cool nights keep the acidity bright; warm days build flavor. Pinot Gris likes slow, steady ripening.

  2. Harvest

    Picked early-to-mid autumn when sugar and acid are balanced. For a crisper style, growers pick earlier; for a richer style, later.

  3. Pressing

    Grapes are gently pressed. Because the skins have a little color, winemakers usually separate the juice quickly to keep the wine pale — but some leave brief skin contact for that signature copper hue.

  4. Fermentation

    Yeast turns grape sugar into alcohol, typically in stainless steel tanks at cool temperatures (12–16°C) to preserve fresh fruit aromas. Richer styles may use old oak barrels.

  5. Aging & blending

    Most Pinot Gris rests on its lees (spent yeast) for a few months to add body and a creamy texture. It’s then racked, lightly filtered, and bottled.

  6. Bottle & release

    Usually released young — within a year of harvest. Best enjoyed within 2–3 years, though top Alsace bottles can age a decade or more.

The benchmark for rich, aromatic Pinot Gris — rounder body, honeyed pear, a hint of spice. Often off-dry.

Food pairing

Perfect with lighter food: grilled white fish, prosciutto and melon, goat cheese, risotto, roast chicken with lemon, Thai curries (for the off-dry Alsatian style). Serve chilled — about 8–10°C — in a medium tulip glass.
