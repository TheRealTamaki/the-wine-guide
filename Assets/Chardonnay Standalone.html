§ 03   A beginner's field guide

Chardonnay

/ˌʃɑːrdəˈneɪ/  ·  "shar-doh-nay"

The world's most recognisable white grape. It wears two very different faces: the lean, mineral crispness of unoaked Chablis and the lush, buttery richness of oaked Napa Valley.

The grape itself

The world's most planted white.

Originating in Burgundy, Chardonnay is a genetic cross of Pinot Noir and Gouais Blanc. Its neutral character makes it a winemaker's canvas: climate and oak shape every bottle differently.

  1. Growing

    Chardonnay buds early, making it vulnerable to spring frosts. It thrives in well-drained limestone and chalk soils, which preserve acidity and mineral expression in the final wine.

  2. Harvest

    Timing is everything. Picking early (cool climates) retains green apple and citrus freshness; picking later (warm climates) develops tropical fruit and higher sugar for fuller-bodied wines.

  3. Pressing

    Whole-cluster or gentle pressing separates juice from skins quickly, keeping the wine pale and clean. Minimal skin contact avoids tannin pickup, preserving the variety's characteristic softness.

  4. Fermentation

    Fermented in stainless steel (for crisp, fruit-forward styles) or in oak barrels (for richness and spice). Wild-yeast ferments add textural complexity and are common in Burgundy and premium New World wines.

  5. Aging & Lees

    Sur lie aging — resting on spent yeast cells — builds a creamy, bread-dough texture called autolysis. Lees stirring (bâtonnage) intensifies this effect, a hallmark of quality white Burgundy.

  6. Oak Aging

    New French oak contributes vanilla, butterscotch, and toasted notes. Unoaked versions use stainless steel or large neutral vessels, keeping the wine fresh and mineral. Malolactic fermentation softens acidity into a buttery roundness.

France

Burgundy

The spiritual home of Chardonnay. Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet, and Chassagne-Montrachet produce the world's most sought-after whites: mineral, complex, and built to age.

Mineral · Nutty · Aged
France

Chablis

The purest expression of unoaked Chardonnay. Kimmeridgian limestone soils produce wines of electric acidity, green apple, and a distinctive flinty, oyster-shell salinity.

Unoaked · Flinty · Crisp
USA — California

Napa & Sonoma

California's signature white: ripe peach and mango fruit amplified by generous oak and full malolactic fermentation. Rich and round, with a creamy texture that defines the "New World" style.

Oaked · Buttery · Tropical
Australia

Margaret River

Western Australia's benchmark. Retains Burgundy-like structure with nectarine and white peach fruit. Margaret River Chardonnay is among the country's most collectible whites.

Elegant · Structured · Peachy
New Zealand

Marlborough

Known more for Sauvignon Blanc, Marlborough also grows restrained, citrus-driven Chardonnay with fine acidity. Hawke's Bay adds riper stone fruit and judicious oak.

Citrus · Fresh · Fine
Chile

Casiblanco

Chile's cool coastal valleys — Casablanca and San Antonio — yield Chardonnays with fresh acidity, green apple, and subtle oak. Outstanding value at every price point.

Food pairing

Serve at 10–13°C in a tulip glass. Unoaked Chardonnay is brilliant with oysters, grilled fish, and sushi. Oaked styles shine alongside roast chicken, lobster with butter, and creamy pasta. Avoid heavy red-meat dishes.
