## What do you actually get each day?

### Day 1: How to taste like you mean it

Three minutes per bottle changes everything. We'll walk through the four steps (look, smell, sip, finish) in the time it takes to open the wine and sit down. The point isn't to sound fancy. It's to start noticing what's in your glass instead of just drinking past it. By the end of Day 1 you'll have tasted one wine you already own and named three things about it you'd never have said yesterday. Our [how to taste wine guide](/wine-basics/how-to-taste-wine/) covers the same four steps in long form if you want a primer before Day 1 lands.

### Day 2: The 5 grapes that cover 80% of restaurant lists

Pinot Noir, Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Riesling. Plus Malbec as the practical sixth. Day 2 is a quick guide to what each one tastes like, where the good versions come from, and which one to order based on what you already drink. After this email, you'll be able to scan any wine list and recognise enough names to make a confident pick.

### Day 3: How to read any wine label in under 10 seconds

Old World labels (region first) versus New World labels (grape first). What the appellation actually tells you. Why "Reserve" means almost nothing in some countries and a lot in others. By the end of Day 3, you'll be able to predict roughly how a wine will taste from the label alone, which is the single biggest jump in confidence in the whole course.

### Day 4: Pairing without a chart (the weight + intensity rule)

Two rules. Match the weight of the wine to the weight of the dish. Match the intensity. That's it. Day 4 walks you through how those rules play out across 12 common meals (steak, salmon, pasta, curry, salad, cheese, pizza, roast chicken, sushi, burgers, dessert, vegetarian) so you can build the same instinct without memorising a chart.

### Day 5: Restaurant ordering scripts that work

The exact phrase that gets you a great bottle inside a price range without sounding cheap. The right way to taste the wine when the server pours that first sip. What to do if the wine is corked. How to handle the wine list when it's 80 pages and your date is watching. By Day 5 you'll have a script for every awkward moment, which means you stop dreading them.

### Day 6: How to spot a faulty bottle and send it back

Most "I just don't like this wine" moments are actually faulty wine. Corked, oxidised, reduced, heat-damaged. Day 6 covers the four faults that show up in real life, what each one smells and tastes like, and the quiet language to send the bottle back without making it weird. This single skill saves you money and saves the night.

### Day 7: Building your "house wine" rotation under $20

Four bottles that handle 90% of the wine you'll drink at home. A weeknight red. A weeknight white. A pasta-and-pizza red. A "friends are over" white. Day 7 names specific grapes, specific regions, specific price points, and tells you which bottle shop chains stock them reliably. By Sunday night you'll have a shopping list that takes the guesswork out of the next month.
