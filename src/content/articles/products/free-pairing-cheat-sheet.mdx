## Why does this cheat sheet work?

Three rules. That's the whole engine. Once you see them, you'll catch yourself applying them to meals that aren't on the sheet.

**Rule 1: Match weight.** A heavy wine and a light dish leave the food invisible. A light wine and a heavy dish leaves the wine invisible. The wine and the food should be in the same heavyweight class. Pinot Noir with salmon. Cabernet with steak. Sauvignon Blanc with salad. The match works because both have similar presence in your mouth.

**Rule 2: Match intensity.** Big flavour wants big flavour. Mild flavour wants mild flavour. A heavily spiced dish needs a wine with enough fruit and aromatic punch to keep up, which is why Riesling and Gewurztraminer crush curry pairings while a quiet Pinot Grigio gets bulldozed. A delicate butter-poached fish wants a wine just as delicate.

**Rule 3: Contrast or echo.** This is the rule that decides between two valid options. You can echo the dish (rich Chardonnay with rich roast chicken, both creamy and round) or you can contrast it (high-acid Sauvignon Blanc with rich goat cheese, the acid cuts through the fat). Both work. Echo feels comforting. Contrast feels alive. Pick based on the mood.

You don't have to memorise these. They're printed on the back of the cheat sheet for the moments when nothing on the front matches what you're cooking.
