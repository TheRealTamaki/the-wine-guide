## Article Structure Template

A typical Wine Guide article follows this structure:

1. **Hook** — Open with something specific (a region, a moment, a surprising fact) — never a generic question
2. **Context** — Why this matters to the reader / when they'd care about this topic
3. **Core content** — Educational meat: regions, varieties, techniques, comparisons
4. **Practical takeaway** — What to buy, try, or look for (affiliate opportunity)
5. **Wrap-up** — Brief, forward-looking — invite them to explore more

---

## Notes

- Wine content should feel like a knowledgeable friend explaining things, not a textbook
- Avoid wine snobbery — the audience is everyday enthusiasts, not sommeliers
- Affiliate mentions should feel natural, not forced — recommend because it's genuinely good
- Seasonal relevance boosts content: pair with holidays, harvest seasons, weather
