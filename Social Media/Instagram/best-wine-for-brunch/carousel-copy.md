## Slide Copy

1. Stop opening the same Prosecco every weekend
   Your eggs, pancakes, bacon, and avocado toast are all asking for different bottles.

2. Pick the wine by the plate first
   Brunch throws maple syrup, hollandaise, bacon, fruit, and toast onto the same table. The smarter move is knowing which bottle belongs with your plate.

3. Match the bottle to what you are actually eating
   Mimosas: dry Prosecco keeps orange juice fresh.
   Pancakes: Moscato can handle maple syrup.
   Eggs Benedict: Chardonnay has the body for hollandaise.
   Bacon and eggs: light Pinot Noir works better than you think.

4. La Marca Prosecco
   Green apple, lemon, and fine bubbles. Mix it three-to-one with fresh orange juice and call brunch handled.

5. Saracco Moscato d'Asti
   Maple syrup beats dry wine flat. This is peachy, gently fizzy, 5.5% ABV, and sweet enough to keep up.

6. Kim Crawford Sauvignon Blanc
   Lime, fresh herbs, and bright acidity cut through avocado and make smoked salmon feel sharper.

7. La Crema Chardonnay
   Creamy texture, lemon, and golden peach. It has enough body for rich eggs without turning heavy.

8. Miraval Rose
   Dry Provence rose with strawberry, white peach, and enough acidity for quiche, fruit, charcuterie, and sunshine.

9. Next weekend, choose like this
   Mimosas: dry Prosecco
   Pancakes or French toast: Moscato d'Asti
   Avocado toast: Sauvignon Blanc
   Eggs Benedict: Chardonnay
   Patio brunch: dry Provence rose
   Bacon and eggs: chilled light Pinot Noir
