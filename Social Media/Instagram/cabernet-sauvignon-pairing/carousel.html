Six pairings that actually work

Foods that make your Cab taste even better

Cab is bold, tannic, and full of dark fruit. The right pairing softens those tannins, lifts the fruit, and turns the whole experience up a level.

Keep swipingthewine.guide
Pairing 0103 / 10
The classic

Red meat

Fat in beef or lamb softens Cab's tannins while the dark cherry and plum notes lock onto the char. A ribeye or rack of lamb is what Cab was literally built for.

Pairing 0204 / 10
The easiest win

Aged cheddar

Fat cuts tannin. Salt amplifies fruit. Grab a block, some crackers, and two minutes later you'll have the best thing you've eaten all week. No cooking required.

Pairing 0305 / 10
The unexpected one

Dark chocolate

Go 70% cacao or higher. Bitter and bold amplify each other in a way that sounds too simple until you try it. One square next to your glass and you'll stop being surprised.

Pairing 0406 / 10
The dinner party move

Mushroom risotto

Earthy wine, earthy food. Cab's savoury, forest-floor notes find a natural home in umami-heavy dishes. It's a combination most guests won't see coming.

→thewine.guide

Pairing 0507 / 10
The cheese board upgrade

Manchego, Parmesan, Pecorino

Any hard, salty aged cheese does the job. Salt and fat round off Cab's tannins and make the fruit feel rounder and bigger. Worth putting all three on the board.

The best of the lot

Herb-crusted lamb

Rosemary and Cabernet Sauvignon is one of wine's great double acts. If you're putting real effort into dinner, this is the combination worth it.
