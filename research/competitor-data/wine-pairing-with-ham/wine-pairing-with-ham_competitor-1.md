# Wines That Pair Ridiculously Well with Ham

Written by Madeline Puckette

#### Basics to Pairing Wine with Ham

**Fruity Wines** – Ham, with its sweet-and-salty richness, pairs best with wines that have a touch of sweetness, plenty of acidity and bold fruit. Great ham-wines include _Riesling, Moscato, Chenin Blanc, Rosé, Lambrusco, Grenache, and Zinfandel._

What wine goes with ham? The general profile of ham is sweet, salty, textured and rich, but you’ll find quite a bit of variation in ham depending on what you get. Because of this, you can pick from a range of wines that work great!

**A Li’l History:** Ham has been a big deal for a very long time _–at least as far back as the time of Gaul._ In the 1700’s, ham was a primary component of the sailor’s diet and demand spread throughout the world.

Today, the pig-based cured meat has quite a range of tastes; from the specially smoked Westphalian hams of Germany (made from acorn-fed pigs), to the thick-sliced deli-counter “honey ham” that you’ve been putting on your _croque-monsieur._

## Pairing Wine with Ham

### Pairing Wine by Style of Ham

There are 3 major production methods of ham, which include salting (dry-curing), wet-curing and smoking. However, since some producers use a combination of all 3 methods, we’ve organized ham by taste (and cited a few examples) so that you can get a better idea of the [flavor pairing](https://winefolly.com/review/taste-flavor-pairing-chart-combinations/ "Techniques for Perfect Taste and Flavor Pairings w/Charts") ideology.

* * *

![Parma Prosciutto Ham Dry-Cured](https://media.winefolly.com/parma-ham.jpg)Perfect plate of Prosciutto di Parma [source](https://www.flickr.com/photos/beckmann/15184440190/ "Prosciutto on Flickr")

#### Dry-Cured, Aged Ham

_Thinly-sliced, bold flavored hams with a chewy texture and typically higher salt content._

A classic example of this style of ham, that nearly all of us know, is the Italian favorite, Prosciutto. You’ll find ham in this style typically served in micro-thin sheets as a melt-in-your mouth appetizer.

- Prosciutto
- Bayonne Ham
- Serrano Ham
- Jamón Ibérico
- Speck

##### Recommended Wines:

The high saltiness factor and dryness of the meat begs for [sparkling wine](https://winefolly.com/tutorial/4-tips-on-finding-awesome-sparkling-wine/ "4 Tips on Finding Awesome Sparkling Wine"). You’ll also do very well with a sparkling Rosé or even a [sparkling red](https://winefolly.com/review/sparkling-red-wine/ "Liquid Caviar: Sparkling Red Wine"). Other great pairing options include still Rosé, crisp minerally and herbaceous white wines, dry Sherry (perhaps a [Fino or Manzanilla Sherry](https://winefolly.com/review/dry-sherry-wine-guide/ "Sherry: The Dry Wine That Everyone Should Love")), and dry Madeira (such as a 5-year [Verdelho or Sercial Madeira](https://winefolly.com/review/what-is-madeira-wine/ "What is Madeira?  The Island Wine")).

![ham-everything-bagel-with-cheese](https://media.winefolly.com/ham-everything-bagel-with-cheese.jpg)Yes please! Blackforest bagelwich [source](https://www.flickr.com/photos/chiropractic/6967686557 "Black forest ham on Flickr")

#### Savory and Smoky Ham

_Medium to thick cut sliced ham with a medium to bold flavor that’s not sweet._

This style is typically smoked but ranges in size from full-sized fresh hams that you bake in the oven to pre-sliced ham that you buy at the grocery store. This less-sweet style of ham could also include processed ham like Mortadella and Spam. Since this style of ham is juicier and not as salty as a dry-cured ham, it is one of the better choices to match with lighter red and [rosé wines](https://winefolly.com/tutorial/guide-styles-of-rose-wine/ "What Rosé Should I Drink?  A Guide to Styles of Rosé Wine").

- Black Forest Ham
- Country-Cured non-glazed Ham
- Smoked Ham Hocks for Soup
- Mortadella
- Spam

##### Recommended Wines:

With less sweetness and more texture, you’ll be delighted with a medium-bodied fruity red with moderate acidity. There is a great range of wines to choose from in this style and here are a few favorites to know: Grenache-based reds (such as the [GSM blend](https://winefolly.com/review/try-gsm-cotes-du-rhone-blend/ "Time to Try ‘GSM’ The Côtes du Rhône Blend")), Zinfandel, new-world style Pinot Noir, Zweigelt ( [from Austria!](https://winefolly.com/review/get-know-austrian-wine-map/ "Get to Know Austrian Wine (with Map)")), Dornfelder (Germany), [deeply-colored Rosé](https://winefolly.com/tutorial/guide-styles-of-rose-wine/ "What Rosé Should I Drink?  A Guide to Styles of Rosé Wine"), The Corvina-based [red wines of Valpolicella](https://winefolly.com/review/valpolicella-wine-pyramid/ "Valpolicella Wine Pyramid from Classico to Amarone"), [Aged Tempranillo](https://winefolly.com/review/rioja-wine-from-crianza-to-gran-reserva/ "Rioja Wine: From Crianza to Gran Reserva"), Nebbiolo, [Sangiovese](https://winefolly.com/review/guide-to-sangiovese-wine/ "Guide to Sangiovese Wine"), Primitivo-Negroamaro blends from Puglia, and “Secco” (dry) [Lambrusco](https://winefolly.com/review/sparkling-red-wine/ "Liquid Caviar: Sparkling Red Wine").

![sweet-canadian-bacon-ham](https://media.winefolly.com/sweet-canadian-bacon-ham.jpg)Canadian Bacon aka “back bacon” [source](https://www.flickr.com/photos/autonome/6818982908/ "Canadian bacon ham on Flickr")

#### Sweet and Salty Ham

_These are typically medium to thick cut hams with a chewy texture and noticeably sweet flavor._

Think Canadian bacon. Since this style of ham is sweet, you’ll want to match with wines that are also sweet or taste very fruity. You might be surprised by some of the recommended pairings but they taste outstanding!

- Canadian Bacon
- Honey Baked Ham
- Glazed Baked Hams
- Mortadella
- Char Siu (Chinese Barbecue Pork–Technically not ham, but fits the flavor profile!)

##### Recommended Wines:

The best way to pair with foods that have sweetness is to have a wine with sweetness too. On the more dry side, pick a [Riesling](https://winefolly.com/review/the-tasters-guide-to-riesling-wine/ "The Taster’s Guide to Riesling Wine"), Chenin Blanc or White Zin/Merlot. On the sweeter side go for [Moscato](https://winefolly.com/review/moscato-wine/ "Discover Moscato Wine"), [Brachetto d’Acqui](https://winefolly.com/review/sparkling-red-wine/ "Liquid Caviar: Sparkling Red Wine"), Vin Santo and [White Port](https://winefolly.com/update/white-port-cocktail-recipe/ "Nightclub Friendly: The White Port Cocktail"). You can probably get away with more new-world style reds including Australian Shiraz, [South African Pinotage](https://winefolly.com/review/give-pinotage-wine-taste/ "Give Pinotage Wine a Chance") and [American Petite Sirah](https://winefolly.com/update/best-red-wine-beginners/ "The Best Red Wine For Beginners").

### Pairing Ideas

Here are a few pairing ideas for wine with ham to get you started.

Hawaiian-Style PizzaCanadian bacon and pineapple may be a kid’s favorite but some of us still love it! This [pizza pairing](https://winefolly.com/tutorial/pizza-wine/ "It’s All About the Sauce: Pairing Wine with Pizza") is ideal with the [sparkling red wine](https://winefolly.com/review/sparkling-red-wine/ "Liquid Caviar: Sparkling Red Wine") Lambrusco. Another great choice would be equal parts cheap red wine and Coca Cola. In Barcelona they call it Kalimotxo (“Cali-Mocho”). Prosciutto and Arugula PizzaThe Italian classic combination deserves a [classic Italian red wine](https://winefolly.com/review/italian-red-wines/ "Use This Flow Chart for Selecting Italian Red Wines"). For this pairing, we love the wines of Sardegna, particularly a red called Cannonau (which is actually Grenache) and Carignano (aka Carignan). They are medium-bodied, leathery, and fruity, and can be found for under $14 a bottle! Croque-Monsieur (Monte Cristo) SandwichThe French were really the ones who started the whole fried-ham-and-cheese-sandwich-thing, which is why we like to call it a _croque-monsieur_ (“croke-mon-sewer”). While this sandwich is outstanding with a racy off-dry Riesling (think Rheingau) we also love the herbal flavors that wines like Sauvignon Blanc, Vermentino, Grüner Veltliner and Verdejo add to this dish. Split Pea and Ham SoupSplit pea is one of the hardest foods to pair with wine because it tends to make wine taste like dirt. However, if you turn to red wines with high acidity like Zweigelt (from Austria) or Valpolicella Classico (from Veneto) you’ll find a match. Another great off-the-wall option that’s surprisingly good is a [dry-style Sercial Madeira.](https://winefolly.com/review/what-is-madeira-wine/ "What is Madeira?  The Island Wine") White Bean & Kale Soup with Smoked Ham HockSaveur has a [drool-worthy recipe](https://www.saveur.com/article/recipes/white-bean-and-lacinato-kale-soup-with-smoked-ham-hock "Saveur soup") for an Italy-inspired soup using ham hock. So, to match the Italian motif of bitter vs. sweet try a Negroamaro or Negroamaro/Primitivo blend from Puglia. Negroamaro translates to “black bitter” but the wines are a perfect balance of dark fruit and [tannin](https://winefolly.com/review/wine-characteristics/ "How Basic Wine Characteristics Help You Find Favorites"). Eggs BenedictThis dish is so rich with creamy hollandaise and sweet ham that you actually need something to cut it. Most opt for orange juice but we think that a Grapefruit and [French Crémant](https://winefolly.com/review/sparkling-wines-france-much-champagne/ "Beyond Champagne: 23 Sparkling Wines of France") Mimosa would be the bees knees.

#### Classic Regional Ham Pairings

[![PDO wine, cheese and meat (protected designation of origin)](https://media.winefolly.com/PDO-Logo.svg_-200x200.png)](https://media.winefolly.com/PDO-Logo.svg_.png) In Europe, there are many different types of ham with Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status. PDO is a classification method similar [to wine](https://winefolly.com/review/looking-for-good-wine-start-with-the-appellation/ "Looking for Good Wine? Start With The Appellation") or cheese (think Parmigiano-Reggiano) that guarantees the regional specialty.

Try local regional wine with it! Here are a few great examples:
