# Romance Defined: Wines for the Sexiest (or Most Depressing) Day of the Year

## Introduction

What truly defines romance? As Valentine's Day approaches, the question becomes impossible to ignore. The author explores whether romance comes from traditional gifts like stuffed animals and chocolates, or if wine, with its sensual descriptors and artisanal qualities, might be the answer.

## The Survey

Taylor consulted wine enthusiasts to identify which wines best embody romance. The responses revealed three clear categories of romantic wines.

## Top Romantic Wine Choices

### Sparkling Wine & Champagne (Leading Choice)

Sparkling wine emerged as the overwhelming favorite. The flirty bubbles and elegant glassware make it naturally romantic. Suggestions included:

- Adding fresh strawberries for sophistication
- Pink Champagne for a rare, delicate option
- Premium bottles like Dom Perignon for guaranteed impressiveness

### Red Wine (Close Second)

Red wine offered a more intimate alternative. Rather than the ephemeral nature of bubbles, red wine invites lingering and slow sipping as it improves over time.

**Recommended varietals:**
- **Cabernet Sauvignon**: Bold, mysterious, gutsy
- **Pinot Noir**: Elegant, feminine, sexy characteristics
- **Syrah**: Spicy and approachable

### Dessert & Sweet Wines

Sweet wines, particularly slightly sweet options rather than cloying varieties, rounded out the selections:

- **Riesling**: Considered the ultimate aphrodisiac
- **Port**: One enthusiast claimed it works 100 percent of the time
- **Moscato d'Asti**: Both sweet and lightly fizzy
