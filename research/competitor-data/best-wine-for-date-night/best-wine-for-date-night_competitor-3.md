# How to Choose the Perfect Wine for a Perfect Date Night

By Matthew Stibbe
Published 15 November 2017 | Updated July 14 2024

## Top Tips for a Perfect Date

1. **Start with bubbly**. Order a glass of sparkling wine as an aperitif rather than rushing into a full bottle selection. The effervescence and crisp acidity refresh the palate without demanding a major commitment. Choose something that appeals to you and make eye contact when toasting.

2. **Communicate**. Ask your date about their preferences. Do they enjoy oaked whites or prefer unoaked varieties? Do they like full-bodied wines or lighter options? For reds, determine if they favor robust or delicate styles. This conversation serves as charming banter while helping you make an informed choice.

3. **Pick your food first**. While the traditional pairing rule suggests white wine with fish, some delicate reds work well with seafood too. Pair spicy dishes with floral wines like Riesling rather than high-alcohol options that amplify heat. Full-bodied reds like Cabernet Sauvignon complement rich meat dishes. If sharing a bottle, select food that accommodates both preferences.

4. **Buy the bottle**. Purchasing a full bottle typically offers better value and selection than individual glasses, unless you're each having only one small pour.

5. **Taste the wine but don't stress out about it**. Verify the label matches your order. Swirl, smell, and sip to check for obvious faults like cork taint or vinegar notes. If everything seems fine, simply acknowledge acceptance. However, "it's okay to return a bottle if it's faulty" by politely requesting an alternative.

6. **Use the price rule of thumb**. Plan to spend at least 50% of your food cost on wine. For special occasions, consider spending more to impress.

7. **Enlist the sommelier's help**. Share your preferences, food selections, budget, and openness to adventurous choices. If discussing price feels uncomfortable, point to wines in your preferred range.

8. **Don't buy the second cheapest wine**. Avoid house wines and the lowest-priced options, as restaurants typically mark these up significantly. Instead, explore non-traditional regions if economizing.

9. **Don't be afraid to play it safe**. Unoaked Chardonnay offers reliable appeal for whites, while elegant Malbec or Merlot provide dependable red options.

10. **Don't be afraid to be adventurous**. Ask the sommelier for recommendations on unusual selections. Wines with difficult names are often overlooked gems, as are those from non-traditional winemaking regions.
