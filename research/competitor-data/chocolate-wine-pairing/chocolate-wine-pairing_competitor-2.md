# How to Pair Wine and Chocolate: Our Definitive Guide

## Introduction

Wine and chocolate enthusiasts can create harmonious combinations by understanding key pairing principles. Eataly's wine experts provide guidance on selecting complementary beverages and confections.

## Five Essential Pairing Principles

### 1. Choose Soft, Velvety Wines

"Silky, velvety, and soft wines are chocolate's best friend." Pairing intense dark chocolates with high-tannin reds can overwhelm the palate. Instead, select wines that are softer and more fruit-forward than the chocolate itself.

### 2. Balance Sweetness Strategically

A wine with fruit-forward characteristics works effectively with chocolate's bitter cocoa notes. The term "sweet" in wine terminology often refers to ripe fruit flavors rather than dessert wines specifically.

### 3. Match Intensity Levels

Lighter chocolates pair well with lighter-bodied wines, while intensely flavored chocolates complement fuller-bodied options with similar weight and style.

### 4. Progress From Light to Dark

Begin tastings with white chocolates and light wines, progressively advancing to richer, darker varieties to avoid palate fatigue.

### 5. Consider Additional Ingredients

Accompaniments like nuts, dried fruit, or caramel influence pairing success. A chocolate containing cranberries might pair nicely with fruit-forward reds featuring berry notes.
