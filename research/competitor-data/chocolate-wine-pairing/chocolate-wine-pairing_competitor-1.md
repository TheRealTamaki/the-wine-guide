# Wine & Chocolate Pairing Guide

## Chocolate Wine Pairing

Fine wine and luxury chocolate pair together beautifully, yet achieving the right balance requires understanding how their flavor profiles interact. Since both contain flavanols with varying acidity and sweetness levels, not all wines complement every chocolate type.

## The Perfect Chocolate & Wine Pairing Guide

When combining these two indulgences, experts note that "the combined levels of tannins in both can cause an unwanted bitter taste" if you pair dark chocolate with dry red wine. Similarly, overly sweet wines with sweet chocolates create an undesirable result.

### Red Wine Pairings

Red wine pairs best with milk chocolate rather than dark varieties. Consider selections like Malbec or Zinfandel, where "the fruitiness and a touch of spice can bring out the flavors of the chocolate." If you prefer dark chocolate with red wine, choose options like Pinot Noir or Cabernet Sauvignon to counterbalance bitterness.

### White Wine Pairings

White wine works wonderfully with white chocolate and flavored milk chocolates. Chardonnay and Sauvignon Blanc "offer a creamy experience when paired with white chocolate or salted milk chocolates." For fruity combinations, try Pinot Grigio or Champagne.

### Dessert Wine Pairings

Sweet dessert wines complement both dark and milk chocolates effectively. Late-harvest Riesling pairs particularly well with nutty chocolates like truffle and ganache. Remember that "the wine should be sweeter than the chocolate you're eating."

### Pairing Considerations

When selecting combinations, account for additional flavors like nuts, fruits, and pralines. Darker chocolates (50%+ cocoa) match fortified wines like Port, while milk chocolates pair with lighter, equally sweet wines. Reserve white chocolate for late-harvest dessert wines or sparkling options such as Prosecco, rosé, or Champagne.
