# Plan the Perfect Wine and Chocolate Tasting: Pairing Tips and Ideas

Whether you're planning a cozy night in, a romantic date, or a sophisticated soiree, pairing the right wine with the right chocolate can elevate the experience.

## Chocolate Peanut Butter

Pair with: Merlot

Chocolate and peanut butter is a classic combination that needs a wine complementing its richness without overpowering it. A Merlot, with its soft tannins and fruity undertones, pairs wonderfully with the creamy texture and salty-sweet flavor of chocolate peanut butter treats.

Also try: Zinfandel or Chardonnay

## Dark Chocolate

Pair with: Cabernet Sauvignon

The intensity of dark chocolate requires a wine that can hold its own. Cabernet Sauvignon, with its bold flavors and firm tannins, is the perfect match for dark chocolate, enhancing its cocoa notes while balancing its bitterness.

Also try: Malbec or Shiraz

## Milk Chocolate

Pair with: Pinot Noir

Milk chocolate, known for its creamy texture and sweet, mellow taste, pairs beautifully with Pinot Noir. This wine's light body and fruity character complement milk chocolate's smoothness, making for a harmonious and indulgent pairing.

Also try: Grenache or Beaujolais

## Milk Chocolate and Caramel

Pair with: Port

Combining milk chocolate with caramel brings out a richness that requires a sweet counterpart like Port. The wine's vibrant fruit flavors and richness mirror the buttery caramel, creating a pairing that is both decadent and delightful.

Also try: Madeira or Sauternes

## Dark Chocolate Sea Salt Caramels

Pair with: Sherry

The complex flavors of dark chocolate sea salt caramels are perfectly matched by Sherry. This wine's nutty and rich profile with hints of sweetness accentuates the caramel and complements the slight bitterness of dark chocolate, all while enhancing the sparkle of sea salt.

Also try: Riesling or Sauternes

## Dark Chocolate Raspberry

Pair with: Rosé

The light, sparkling Rosé, with its natural sweetness and hints of berries, pairs perfectly with the fruity sharpness of dark chocolate raspberry, making each bite a fresh and vibrant experience.

Also try: Prosecco or Lambrusco

## Milk Chocolate Pretzels

Pair with: Riesling

The salty crunch of pretzels coated in smooth milk chocolate goes splendidly with Riesling. The wine's sweetness and acidity cut through the salt while elevating the chocolate, resulting in a perfectly balanced bite.

Also try: Moscato or Gewurztraminer

## Milk Chocolate Almond Toffee

Pair with: Chardonnay

Milk chocolate almond toffee blends the creamy sweetness of milk chocolate with the rich crunch of almonds and the buttery taste of toffee. Chardonnay, with its deep nutty flavors, complements this combination by enhancing both the nuttiness of the almonds and the complexity of the toffee.

Also try: Sherry or Port

## Dark Chocolate Chewy Coconut

Pair with: Moscato d'Asti

The exotic taste of chewy coconut embedded within dark chocolate calls for a wine that echoes its tropical flair. Moscato d'Asti, with its light body, gentle bubbles, and floral sweetness, complements the rich coconut flavor without overwhelming the palate.

Also try: Riesling or Sauternes

## Caramel and Chocolate Dipped Apples

Pair with: Chardonnay

Enjoying a caramel and chocolate-dipped apple with Chardonnay is a natural pairing. Chardonnay enhances the apple's natural flavors while adding a sophisticated boozy note that complements the layers of caramel and chocolate.

Also try: Apple Cider (hard cider) or Ice Wine

## Milk Chocolate Coffee Caramel

Pair with: Port

The deep, roasted flavors of coffee caramel blended with the silky texture of milk chocolate are perfectly matched by Port. Its profound sweetness and strong coffee-like undertones echo the rich profile of the chocolate.

Also try: Sherry or an Espresso Martini (for a cocktail twist)

Whether you're a seasoned connoisseur or not, this guide aims to inspire you to gather friends, pour some wine, and indulge in the goodness of wine and chocolate pairings.
