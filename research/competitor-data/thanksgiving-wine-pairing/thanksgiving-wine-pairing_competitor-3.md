# Wines for Thanksgiving: Pairing Guide for a Classic Thanksgiving Feast

## Overview

The article provides wine pairing recommendations for various Thanksgiving dishes, authored by Diana Zahuranec and published November 5, 2021.

## Key Recommendations by Course

**If Serving Only One Wine:**
A Gamay-based Beaujolais-Villages offers versatility across appetizers, turkey, sides, and carbohydrates.

**Appetizers:** Champagne Taittinger Brut La Francaise, described as a delicate blend of 40% Chardonnay, 35% Pinot Noir, and 25% Pinot Meunier, complements cheese boards and various starters.

**Stuffing:** Cotes-du-Rhone with expressive notes of floral and spice pairs well with herb-laden dressing.

**Turkey:** Chateau d'Aqueria Tavel Rose, a full-bodied French rose, works with both dark and light meat.

**Roasted Squash:** Merlot's layers of red fruit flavor and savory spices complement roasted vegetable sides.

**Green Vegetables:** A Vermentino offering zesty, herbaceous, and mineral qualities serves as a palate cleanser.

**Carbohydrates:** Pinot Noir balances complexity with universal appeal for rolls and potatoes.

**Dessert:** Tawny or Ruby Port complements pie courses.

## About the Author

Diana Zahuranec holds WSET 2 certification and has worked in wine since 2012, with background in anthropology and food culture studies.
