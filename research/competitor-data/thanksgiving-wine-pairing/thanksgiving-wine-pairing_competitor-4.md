# How to Pair Wines with Thanksgiving Dinner

By Kat Odell, 24 Nov 2025, 7 Minute read

## Introduction

Wine has long been central to the author's family traditions. She inherited her father's passion for wine and eventually took over holiday wine-selection responsibilities. This guide shares her approach to selecting bottles that complement Thanksgiving's diverse menu.

## How to Choose Bottles

The author emphasizes that wine pairing is subjective, as everyone has different tastes. Her personal preferences include brioche-y Champagne, red and white Burgundy, oxidative wines like dry sherry, and really anything that is simply delicious.

Since guests have varying preferences, she recommends opening multiple bottles to provide options.

## Starting the Meal with Lighter Wines

For the cheese course, lighter, cheese-friendly wines work best. The author suggests:

- Ramiro Ibanez's UBE Miraflores Palomino (Southern Spain): A saline wine with oxidative qualities that cuts through cheese richness
- Xander Soren's Olivet Lane (California Central Coast): A smooth Pinot with dried strawberry, cassis, and black pepper notes
- Granville's Holstein Vineyard (Willamette Valley, Oregon): Another excellent Pinot option

## What to Pour with Dinner

Thanksgiving calls for bolder wines that handle the meal's layered richness and brown spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and clove.

**White wine selections:**
- Quinta Nova's Mirabilis (Portugal): Full-bodied with toasted almond, vanilla, and clove
- Early Mountain's Petit Manseng (Virginia): Intense yet elegant, with brioche and pear notes

**Red wine selections:**
- Henri & Philippe Jouan's Gevrey-Chambertin Aux Echezeaux (Burgundy): A feminine wine with plum and cassis flavors
- Sequoia Grove's Cabernet Sauvignon (Napa): Satiny and concentrated, with dried currants and blackberry
- Aperture's Oliver Ranch Cabernet Sauvignon (Napa): An alternative California option

## Save Room for Something Sweet

Desserts like pumpkin and apple pie pair well with wines offering both sweetness and acidity.

**Dessert wine recommendation:**
- Grgich Hills Late Harvest (Sauternes-style): Made from Sauvignon Blanc, Riesling, and Gewurztraminer, featuring floral and honeyed notes with hints of orange

The author notes that Late-harvest Riesling or Sauternes are classics for autumnal desserts, echoing warm spices without overwhelming them.
