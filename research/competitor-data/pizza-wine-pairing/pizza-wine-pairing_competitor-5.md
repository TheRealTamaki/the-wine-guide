# Best Wine With Pizza: Easy Pairing Guide

## What Wine With Pizza? The Ultimate Pizza and Wine Guide

Wine pairing with pizza creates an exceptional dining experience. Both items shine independently, but together they elevate the meal significantly. Understanding how to match wines with specific pizza types requires knowledge of flavor profiles and ingredients.

### Key Points

- Pizza pairs successfully with both red and white wines
- The ideal wine depends on pizza toppings and sauce composition
- Bold reds like Zinfandel or Syrah complement pepperoni and meat pizzas
- Light reds such as Pinot Noir work well with mushroom or Margherita varieties
- White wines like Sauvignon Blanc suit veggie or pesto pizzas
- Creamy white pizzas pair best with Chardonnay
- Sparkling wines and rosé offer versatility across most pizza types
- Avoid overly sweet or heavy wines unless the pizza features sweet or spicy elements

## Best Wine Pairings by Pizza Type

| Pizza Type | Recommended Wine | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Margherita | Chianti or Pinot Grigio | Balances tomato acidity and fresh basil |
| Pepperoni | Syrah or Zinfandel | Bold enough to match spicy, salty meat |
| Meat Lovers | Cabernet Sauvignon | Stands up to rich, savory meats |
| Veggie | Sauvignon Blanc | Bright and crisp to match lighter toppings |
| White Pizza | Chardonnay | Rich and creamy to complement the cheese base |
| BBQ Chicken | Malbec or Shiraz | Fruity wines enhance BBQ sweetness and smoke |

### Sausage Pizza Pinotage or Syrah

Sausage pizza features bold spices including oregano, thyme, anise, and fennel. These seasonings pair excellently with full-bodied wines. Pinotage and Syrah offer olive, plum, blackberry, and blueberry notes that complement the sausage's dark flavor profile. This combination creates a memorable tasting experience.

### Margherita Pizza Dry Rosé or Garnacha

This Neapolitan-style pizza combines fresh basil, mozzarella cheese, tomato sauce, garlic, and olive oil. Dry Rosé provides black grape flavors that balance these ingredients beautifully. Alternatively, Garnacha, a light red wine, delivers grape-based flavors that work equally well with this classic preparation.

### Pepperoni Pizza Cabernet Franc or Sangiovese

Pepperoni contains cured meats, sugar, paprika, anise, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper. These bold, spicy flavors require strong wines to achieve balance. French Cabernet Franc or Italian Sangiovese both possess the intensity needed to stand up to pepperoni's rich, pronounced flavors.

### Cheese Pizza GSM or Montepulciano

Red sauce on cheese pizza carries strong flavors requiring equally robust wines. GSM (Grenache, Syrah, Mourvèdre blend) combines three grape varieties for comprehensive flavor matching. Montepulciano d'Abruzzo offers fruity, dry characteristics with acidic notes from plum, boysenberry, wild cherry, and blackberry.

### Hawaiian Pizza Lambrusco, Riesling, or Zinfandel

This combination of bacon or ham, pineapple, mozzarella cheese, and cherry tomato sauce needs a wine with acidity and balance. Riesling functions as an excellent palette cleanser. Zinfandel provides a robust, dry alternative for red wine preferences. Lambrusco offers a third option for variety.

### White Pizza Chardonnay or Pinot Noir

Without red sauce, white pizza depends on creamy cheese flavors. Pinot Noir and Chardonnay both enhance cheese-forward dishes. Chardonnay's rich and creamy characteristics make it particularly suitable for this style.

### Salad Pizza Grüner Veltliner, Sauvignon Blanc, or Verdejo

Vegetarian pizzas require wines with crisp, bright characteristics. Grüner Veltliner delivers tangy citrus, apricot, and grapefruit notes. Sauvignon Blanc provides passion fruit, lime, green apple, and peach flavors. Verdejo rounds out options with melon, apple, peaches, and pear profiles.

### Barbecue Chicken Pizza Malbec or Touriga Nacional

Barbecue sauce demands fruity red wines. Malbec offers jammy flavor with raisin, raspberry, plum, black cherry, and pomegranate notes. Touriga Nacional from Portugal contributes leathery, dark flavors that complement barbecue's smoky character.

### Anchovy Pizza Alvarinho or Assyrtiko

Anchovies provide rich, salty, umami flavors enhanced by oregano, capers, tomatoes, and garlic. Assyrtiko (Greek white wine) delivers citrus, saline, flint, and passionfruit characteristics. Alvarinho (Galician white wine) offers peach and apricot alternatives.

## Conclusion on Best Wine With Pizza

Understanding wine and pizza compatibility transforms your dining experience. With the perfect glass of the right wine, you can elevate the taste of your favorite pizza to uncharted heights. Focus on matching the ingredients and flavors used in your pizza selection, then choose wines with complementary characteristics.

## FAQs on Wine with Pizza

**Question: What is the best red wine to drink with pizza?**

Bold reds like Zinfandel, Syrah, and Cabernet Sauvignon suit meat-heavy pizzas. For lighter preparations, Pinot Noir or Chianti work better.

**Question: Can you drink white wine with pizza?**

Yes. Sauvignon Blanc and Chardonnay pair well with veggie pizzas, white sauce varieties, and lighter toppings.

**Question: What wine goes best with pepperoni pizza?**

Zinfandel, Syrah, or Malbec balance the spice and fat of pepperoni effectively.

**Question: Is rosé a good wine to pair with pizza?**

Yes, rosé offers versatility across many pizza styles, particularly Margherita, veggie, and white pizzas.

**Question: Do I need to pair expensive wine with pizza?**

No. Affordable wines work excellently with pizza. Prioritize flavor balance over price when selecting.

**Question: Can I pair sparkling wine with pizza?**

Absolutely. Sparkling wine pairs well with salty, cheesy, or fried toppings and provides refreshing contrast to rich flavors.
