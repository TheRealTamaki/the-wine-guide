# The Best Styles of Wine to Pair With Pizza Margherita

## The Origin and History of the Famous Pizza

Pizza Margherita draws inspiration from Italy's national flag colors and is named after Queen Margherita of Savoy, who reigned from 1878 to 1900. Chef Raffaele Esposito created the dish in June 1889 at Pizzeria di Pietro in Naples during a royal visit. A commemorative plaque marks this historic moment.

However, historical records suggest the pizza's origins predate this story. According to research, pizzas with identical toppings existed around 1830. The pizza became famous through the Queen's endorsement rather than being invented specifically for her.

## Pizza Margherita Wine Pairings

Traditional Italian Pizza Margherita features "a fabulous crunchy crust and uses high-quality farm-fresh ingredients." This simple yet elegant combination deserves wine selections that complement rather than overpower its delicate flavors.

### Sangiovese

Chianti, produced from Sangiovese grapes in Tuscany, represents the premier red wine pairing. The wine's acidity mirrors the tomato component perfectly. Entry-level Chianti bottles offer bright acidity with tart red fruit notes, while premium Chianti Classico options deliver fuller body with riper fruit characteristics and firmer tannins.

### Pinot Grigio

White wine enthusiasts should consider Italian Pinot Grigio, particularly versions from Alto Adige, Trentino, and Friuli-Venezia regions. These light, high-acidity white wine selections cut through cheese richness effectively, similar to how lemon complements seafood preparations.

### Metodo Classico

Sparkling wines made using traditional methods offer excellent alternatives. These dry, high-acidity selections reference the pizza's crust through brioche qualities developed during lees contact. Trentodoc DOC and Franciacorta DOC represent quality Italian options.
