# A Guide to Wine Pairing With Sushi

## What Wine Goes With Sushi?

Fish dishes pair best with wines featuring "bright acidity and citrus notes to cut through the buttery, fatty meat and bring out its authentic flavors." Light red wines may work, but their tannins typically overwhelm delicate fish preparations.

## Rice Wine (Sake)

Sake represents a traditional Japanese pairing option, offering nutty flavors and smooth textures that harmonize with sushi across all styles.

## White Wines

High-acid, low-tannin white wines are ideal since reds typically overpower light fish. Recommended options include:

### Riesling
Balanced acidity with apricot, stone fruit, and apple notes enhance fatty raw fish. Dry versions pair with salmon and shrimp nigiri, while off-dry styles complement spicy rolls.

### Grüner Veltliner
Featuring lemon, lime, white pepper, and green pea notes, this wine pairs excellently with cucumber, avocado, and eel, particularly dragon and avocado rolls.

### Sauvignon Blanc
This light-bodied, citrus-forward option works beautifully with white fish, vegetarian rolls, and shrimp tempura, cutting through fatty components effectively.

### Pinot Grigio
With lemon, lime, apple, and stone fruit flavors, this crisp white balances any sushi dish, especially sashimi, shrimp, and California rolls.

## Sparkling Wines

Champagne and Prosecco offer delicate, minerally profiles with subtle toastiness. These wines cleanse the palate effectively and should be selected in drier styles.

## Rosé Wines

Provence Rosé, with mineral finishes and fresh flower and red fruit notes, pairs exceptionally with California rolls and seafood preparations.

## Best Red Wine With Sushi

### Pinot Noir
Light-bodied with minimal tannins, Pinot Noir suits fattier fish like tuna and salmon, particularly Philadelphia rolls with smoked salmon and cream cheese.

## Summary

Dry to off-dry, light to medium-bodied white wines with citrus and floral aromatics offer reliable pairings. Alternatively, dry Rosé, sparkling wines, or select low-tannin reds provide variety.
