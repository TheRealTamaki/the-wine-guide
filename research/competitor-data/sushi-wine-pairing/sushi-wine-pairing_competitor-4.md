# What Wines Pair Best With Sushi?

**Date:** June 28, 2022
**By:** Matt Tervooren

Sushi presents unique pairing challenges due to its delicate flavors and intense accompaniments like wasabi, soy sauce, and ginger. The solution involves selecting wines with complementary characteristics rather than fighting against them.

## The Best Wines To Drink With Sushi

### Sake

The most authentic choice comes from the same region as the dish. Sake offers "crisp, with refreshing saltiness that'll remind you of coastal white wines," making it an excellent traditional pairing.

**Recommendation:** Akabu, Junmai ($45)

### Coastal White Wines

Look for textured wines with mineral qualities, particularly Etna Bianco or Canary Island varieties that mirror the ocean's influence on sushi.

**Recommendation:** Coastal Whites 3-Pack ($100)

### Chablis

High acidity and salty minerality make Chablis ideal for raw fish. The region's ancient seabed geology and fossilized oyster shells create a thematic connection to the ocean.

**Recommendation:** Dauvissat, Petit Chablis 2019 ($95)

### Champagne

Bubbles cleanse the palate between bites while acidity cuts through fish richness. This pairing works particularly well with fried preparations like tempura.

**Recommendation:** Laherte Freres, Extra Brut Champagne Les Empreintes 2014 ($95)

### Riesling

Piercing acidity handles rich fish preparations, while subtle sweetness balances spicy elements like wasabi and spicy mayo, ideal for mixed sushi orders.

**Recommendation:** Philip Lardot, Riesling der Bauer Trocken 2019 ($65)

### Rosé

Red wine's tannins create metallic flavors with fish, but rosé offers structure without this drawback. Choose bottles balanced enough for bold sushi yet light enough for delicate preparations.

**Recommendation:** Clos Cibonne Tibouren, Rosé Provence 2020 ($45)
