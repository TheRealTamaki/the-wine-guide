# Which Wines Pair Well with Sushi?

## Introduction

Fermented beverages complement sushi exceptionally well. While sake remains the traditional choice due to its rice base, wine offers interesting pairing possibilities depending on the specific sushi ingredients and preparation methods.

## Red Wine Pairings

Matured red wines work best with sushi featuring bold flavors and sweet glazes. According to the article, "toppings that use strong seasonings like Nikiri, including tuna and conger eel with sweet filling, match superbly with matured red wines."

**Recommended red wines:**
- Pinot Noir varieties
- Bourgogne Chambolle Musigny
- Cote de Beaune
- Morey-Saint-Denis

## White Wine Pairings

Lighter white wines pair well with delicate fish preparations. "White wines such as a lighter Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc and Riesling go well with white fish flavored with Citrus sudachi and yuzu or squid eaten with salt."

**Recommended white wines:**
- Chardonnay
- Sauvignon Blanc
- Riesling
- Bourgogne Chablis

## Challenging Pairings

Neither traditional red nor white wines pair effectively with herring or salmon roe. The article explains that iron content in wine contributes to undesirable fishy flavors when combined with fish eggs.

**Better alternatives for roe:**
- Sherry (Spain)
- Champagne (France)
- Cava (Spain)
- Franciacorta (Italy)

These options contain lower iron levels, minimizing the fishy taste.
