# Summer Wine for Every Activity on Your List

By Kat | June 9, 2023

## Introduction

As summer arrives with its relaxed pace, the author explores wine pairings for various warm-weather activities. All recommended wines are priced at $26 or less, making them accessible options for different summer scenarios from beach days to backyard gatherings.

## Beach Vibes: Rosé

Rosé stands out as the quintessential summer wine. The author prefers versions with ripe summer red berry fruit as well as tropical fruit balanced by floral and mineral notes.

Recommended selections:
- DAOU Discovery Rosé ($18). Features stone fruits and melon with bright acidity
- Leyda Rosé of Pinot Noir ($20). Cool climate offering with ruby grapefruit, peach, and salinity
- Chateau Miraval Rosé ($20). Elegant with berries, apples, watermelon, and floral characteristics

## Sailboat Vibes: Bubbly

For boating occasions, sparkling wines offer affordability without sacrificing quality.

Notable options:
- Codorníu ARS Collecta Cava ($15). Available in both Brut and Rosé varieties
- Gruet Blanc De Blancs Sauvage ($21). New Mexico producer offering chalk, citrus, and brioche notes
- Domaine Ste Michelle Brut Rosé ($13-15). Washington wine with strawberry and raspberry profiles

## Hiking Vibes: Malbec

Red wine requiring no refrigeration makes sense for outdoor adventures. Malbec's smooth character suits post-hike relaxation.

Selections:
- Catena High Mountain Vines Malbec ($15). From Mendoza, precise and balanced
- Graffigna Glorious Selection Malbec ($20). Features raspberries, currants, spice, and florals

## Lakeside Vibes: Sauvignon Blanc

The author reconsidered her initial skepticism about Sauvignon Blanc, discovering affordable options beyond pricey Loire Valley bottles.

Recommendations:
- Rodney Strong Charlotte's Home ($11). Tropical with citrus and melon
- The Paring Sauvignon Blanc ($25). Santa Ynez blend utilizing premium vineyard fruit
- DAOU Sauvignon Blanc ($17). Combines minerals with stone fruits and honeydew

## Backyard BBQ Vibes

Grilling occasions call for red wines that complement smoked meats.

Featured wines:
- The Paring Red ($26). Bordeaux blend with mocha and black fruit
- Château de Lancyre Pic Saint-Loup ($26). Languedoc red offering bold fruit with herbs
- Ciffre Saint-Chinian ($23). Grenache-dominant blend with cherry jam and tobacco notes

The author emphasizes Languedoc's emerging reputation for quality wines at accessible prices, particularly its commitment to organic production.
