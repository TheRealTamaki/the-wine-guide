# Summer Wine Recommendations

by admin | Jul 2, 2025 | Blog

## Whites, Rosés, and Reds for the Grill

The author recently relocated to New Mexico after spending over 30 years in San Francisco. Unlike the Bay Area's cool summers, New Mexico offers hot days with significant temperature drops at night due to elevation. This climate creates an ideal backdrop for outdoor grilling and wine enjoyment.

The author describes their New Mexican-style home with a gas grill capable of reaching 600 degrees quickly. While outdoor dining on the patio is possible, summer heat and aggressive wasps sometimes make indoor dining preferable. However, a bug zapper may help solve this year's pest issues.

## Summer Wine Guidelines and Recommendations

### I. Unoaked, High-Acid White Wines

These versatile wines pair well with various dishes. Examples include Riesling, Sauvignon Blanc, Albariño, Grüner Veltliner, and Italian varieties.

**Specific recommendations:**
- 2023 Craggy Range Sauvignon Blanc (Marlborough)
- 2023 Inama Soave Classico (Veneto)
- Dönnhoff Weissburgunder
- 2022 Punta Crena Pigato (Liguria)

### II. Dry Rosés

"The most versatile summer wines" that work with lighter dishes and grilled proteins alike.

**Specific recommendations:**
- 2024 Bieler et Fils Coteau d'Aix en Provence
- 2023 Schloss Gobelsburg Zweigelt Rosé (Kamptal)
- 2023 Txomin Etxaniz Rosado Txakoli
- 2022 Domaine de Fontsainte Gris de Gris Rosé (Corbières)

### III. Lighter Reds

These can be served chilled and complement picnic fare and grilled items. Examples: Pinot Noir, Gamay, Sangiovese, Barbera, and lighter Grenache blends.

**Specific recommendations:**
- 2022 Ridge Three Valleys Zinfandel (Sonoma County)
- 2022 Jean Foillard Beaujolais-Villages
- 2022 Paolo Scavino Barbera d'Alba
- 2023 Argyle Pinot Noir (Willamette Valley)

### IV. Richer Reds

These pair well with grilled dishes when they feature plenty of fruit and not too much tannin.

**Specific recommendations:**
- 2022 Yalumba Shiraz (Barossa)
- 2021 Susanna Balbo Signature Malbec (Mendoza)
- 2022 Juan Gil Monastrell Silver Label (Jumilla)

## Serving Temperature Advice

Temperature significantly affects summer wine enjoyment. The author recommends serving whites and rosés colder than usual since they warm quickly outdoors. Lighter reds should also be chilled, and adding ice to glasses remains acceptable if wines become too warm.

## Final Thoughts

The author concludes: Drink whatever you want out of whatever you want with whatever you're having to eat. Context matters more than rules, personal preference trumps tradition.
