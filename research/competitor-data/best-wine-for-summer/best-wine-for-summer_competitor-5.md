# What is the Quintessential Summer Red Wine?

Now is the time to drink light, refreshing reds with a chill. Here are some of our favorites.

By Jon Bonné | August 24, 2016

## The Case for Summer Reds

During a summer tasting in Brooklyn, the PUNCH team gathered to evaluate their favorite chillable red wines. As the author notes, you can't live on rosé alone, yet there's a genuine need for wines offering a deeper sense of fruit, a bit of tannin for food while remaining suitable for an ice-filled cooler.

Light red wines were once considered novelties, but attitudes have shifted. The era of demanding heavy, full-bodied reds has given way to appreciation for lighter expressions that don't sacrifice complexity or substance.

## Trending Light Reds

The article highlights several wine regions gaining popularity for summer-appropriate reds:

- Grignolino and ruchè from Piedmont
- Frappato from Sicily
- Schiava from northern Italy
- Gamay in all forms
- Loire Valley reds
- Southern German wines

These selections represent a deliberate departure from the insecure cabernet drinkers who once dismissed lighter wines.

## Top Summer Red Selections

2014 Broc Cellars Solano County Green Valley Valdiguié | $26

Valdiguié, a rediscovered southern French grape, offers punchy fruit with dark purple and sour cherry notes plus violet nuances. The wine exemplifies California's ability to produce refreshing, fruit-forward reds.

2014 Enderle & Moll Baden Pinot Noir | $28

This German producer creates ethereally light pinots featuring sour berries, intense mineral qualities, and herbal notes, qualities once seen as character flaws in German reds.

2014 Weingut Knauss Württemberg Trollinger | $20

Trollinger demonstrates how a grape whose lightness was once considered a character fault can deliver both structure and refreshment, with hibiscus and wild strawberry characteristics reminiscent of agua fresca.

2015 Crivelli Castagnole Monferrato Ruché | $23

This Piedmontese producer specializes in lighter red grapes, offering an intensely floral wine with geranium aromatics, cherry fruit, and subtle bitterness.

## Important Tasting Insights

A crucial observation: when red wines are chilled, putting a chill on a red only highlights its shortcomings. Raw tannins become more grating, and unbalanced acidity grows jarring, meaning quality matters more in summer reds.

The article also notes that some wines initially seemed uninteresting during the blind tasting, requiring a second evaluation to properly assess their character.
