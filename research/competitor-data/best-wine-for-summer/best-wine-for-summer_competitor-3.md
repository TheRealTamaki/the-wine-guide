# 5 Best Wines to Drink on a Beach Day... that aren't bubbles!

By Angelica Nohra
Wine Importer | Wine List Consultant | Speaker

## The Beach Day Wine Rules

When selecting wines for a beach outing, the author follows specific criteria: screw-cap bottles to prevent sand contamination, easy-drinking qualities for extended consumption, and wines that maintain their flavor when chilled.

## The Five Recommendations

1. Rosé

The top choice for beach days. The author advocates for dry rosés with fruity notes, specifically recommending their Petula and Les Artz varieties. Both feature screw caps and possess character despite their accessibility.

2. Beaujolais/Gamay

For those preferring red wine, chilled Gamay offers a lighter alternative. These dirty, fun reds provide enough tannin to complement cheese while remaining refreshing in summer heat.

3. Lambrusco/Sweeter Red Wines

Lower-alcohol, lightly fizzy options work well for those avoiding tannins. The Primavera Rosso serves as the author's current recommendation, medium-sweet and screw-capped.

4. Aromatic White Wines

Pinot Gris, Sauvignon Blanc, and Albariño shine at the beach. The author particularly favors Albariño for its herbaceous qualities, suggesting it pairs naturally with the ocean environment.

5. Beer

Practical and refreshing, stubbies served cold complement the Australian beach experience. Lighter styles like pale ales and lagers work best.
