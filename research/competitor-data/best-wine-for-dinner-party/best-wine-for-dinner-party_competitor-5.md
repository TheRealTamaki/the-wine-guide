# What Wine Should I Serve at a Dinner Party? Ask the Sommelier

## Sommelier Rudina Arapi responds:

When hosting a dinner party, sommelier Rudina Arapi, head sommelier at London's Galvin at Windows, emphasizes that guest preferences should be your primary consideration. She notes, you want to make sure your guests are happy, so it's important to note their preferences as well as your own.

### Key Considerations

**Guest Preferences and Seasonal Factors**

Beyond individual tastes, consider the season and weather. Light rose suits summer alfresco dining better than heavy, tannin-laden reds. However, Arapi stresses that personal enjoyment matters most. A perfect pairing means nothing if you dislike the recommended wine style.

**Food Pairing Philosophy**

While thoughtful wine-food pairing enhances a meal, Arapi recommends prioritizing what you and your guests genuinely enjoy. She suggests adapting recommendations when necessary; if your guests prefer white wine with their meat course, opt for a fuller-bodied white with buttery, oaky characteristics.

### Recommended Wine Selection

Offer variety throughout the meal:
- **Aperitif:** Champagne to start
- **White wines:** German Riesling (lighter, citrusy) followed by white Burgundy (creamier, more substantial)
- **Red wines:** Monovarietal options like Pinot Noir, Nebbiolo, Barolo, Barbaresco, or Brunello di Montalcino
- **Dessert:** Port, Madeira, Tokaji, or Monbazillac

### Guest-Brought Bottles

When guests offer wine as gifts, Arapi recommends opening them to share. This moment reveals everyone's collective selections and creates opportunities for discovery.

### Bringing Wine to Others' Parties

When invited elsewhere, Arapi suggests bringing something with personal significance. Perhaps a wine from travels or a passionate recommendation you can discuss with your hosts.
