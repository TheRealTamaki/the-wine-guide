# How to Choose Wine for a Party Without Stress

## Overview

Hosting a celebration can feel daunting when selecting wine, but you don't need professional expertise to make smart choices. The article, authored by sommelier Blake Leland, offers practical guidance for serving wine that satisfies guests while keeping the selection process straightforward.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Prioritize Accessibility

Stick to easy crowd-pleasers, think Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay for white, Pinot Noir or Cabernet Sauvignon for red. These varieties are familiar, versatile with food, and widely appreciated across different palates.

### 2. Calculate Quantities Thoughtfully

The guide suggests planning approximately half a bottle per guest for a 2 to 3 hour event. Since one bottle yields 4 to 5 glasses, hosting 10 people requires roughly 5 to 6 bottles. It's preferable to have surplus than to run short.

### 3. Offer Variety

Include at least one red and one white option. Adding sparkling wine creates festive atmosphere, while rose serves as an approachable middle option year-round.

### 4. Match the Occasion's Tone

Different settings call for different selections:
- **Casual outdoor gatherings:** Zinfandel, Malbec, or chilled rose
- **Formal dinners:** Bordeaux blends, premium Chardonnay, or Champagne
- **Relaxed events:** Budget-friendly, approachable blends

### 5. Don't Overspend

Quality wines between $12 and $20 typically satisfy most guests. Purchasing extra bottles you personally enjoy ensures your wine collection expands if bottles remain unopened.

## Final Takeaway

A great party is about the people, not the wine list. Thoughtful planning and relaxed confidence matter more than expensive selections.
