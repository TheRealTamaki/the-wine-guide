# Dinner Party Wines: A Guest's Guide to What to Bring

## Introduction

As hosting season arrives, guests are encouraged to elevate their wine contributions beyond last-minute supermarket selections. This guide outlines strategies for selecting and presenting bottles that will enhance your standing at the dinner table.

## Best Bottles to Bring

### Crowd Pleasers

The foundation of dinner party wine selection involves choosing approachable wines that strike the middle ground when it comes to tannin, body, acidity and alcohol.

**Red Wine Tips:**
- Select medium-bodied options like Rioja or Sangiovese that emphasize flavor over tannin
- Pinot Noir from Austria, California, or New Zealand offers character without breaking the bank
- For foodie groups, consider Nebbiolo from Langhe
- Cotes-du-Rhone Villages provides reliable quality
- Avoid predictable Cabernet Sauvignon in favor of Austrian varietals like Zweigelt

**White Wine Tips:**
- Avoid watery whites without a personality in favor of quality selections
- Select Pinot Grigio from specific regions (Friuli, Alto-Adige) rather than budget options
- Understand that Chardonnay varies dramatically by region: research before purchasing
- Introduce guests to less common varieties: Albarino, Gruner Veltliner, Etna Bianco, or Eastern European Riesling
- Approach orange wines cautiously unless your group enjoys experimentation

### Pairing Wine to the Meal

Contact your host beforehand to learn the menu, allowing you to select wines with complementary profiles. If details remain unclear, choose bottles featuring bright fruit flavours, good acidity and relatively low tannins.

### Sparkling Wine Strategy

Avoid inexpensive Prosecco unless you're willing to invest in Grower or small production Champagne or premium DOCG Prosecco. Budget options risk contributing to faux-fancy-fatigue among hosts accustomed to casual fizz.

## Best Practices for Bringing Wine

**Temperature Matters**
Arrive with pre-chilled white wine to avoid rummaging through the host's freezer and to project preparedness.

**The Double-Bottle Approach**
Bring two bottles: one for immediate consumption and one designated as a gift, clearly labeled to prevent confusion during the evening.

**Placement Protocol**
Don't position your bottle prominently at dinner; embrace the principle that contributions become communal property.

**Commentary Restraint**
Share your bottle's story enthusiastically, but avoid the temptation to comment on anyone else's contribution beyond praising the host's selections.

## Going the Extra Mile

**Spirits and Cocktails**
Elevate your contribution by supplying pre-mixed cocktails (requiring only ice and garnish) or premium digestifs. Options like coffee brandy served over ice appeal broadly.

**Meaningful Selection**
Choose bottles connected to shared memories: wines from regions you've visited together or producers discovered at previous tastings demonstrate thoughtfulness beyond generic selection.

## Where to Source Quality Bottles

Local wine retailers with knowledgeable staff provide superior guidance compared to supermarket shopping, offering expertise that broadens your wine education while ensuring reliable recommendations.
