# Wine Pairings for Mexican Food: Expert Tips from Vintner Caren Rideau

## Overview

Kitchen designer and vintner Caren Rideau shares guidance on pairing wines with Mexican cuisine, drawing from her book *Caren Rideau: Kitchen Designer, Vintner, Entertaining at Home*. She operates Tierra Y Vino winery in Santa Ynez Valley alongside her partner, Andres Ibarra.

## Wine Pairing Principles

**White Wine Strategy**
Rideau seeks "a balance of fruit and acid, which creates a lasting finish" ideal for seafood and spicy dishes. The acidity cleanses the palate, similar to how lime brightens carne asada.

**Red Wine Approach**
For red wines, she focuses on tannins, compounds that create dryness in the throat. These work well with heartier dishes like red meat. As wine ages, tannins dissipate, allowing fruit flavors to become more prominent.

## Flavor-Matching Method

Rather than memorizing complex wine terminology, Rideau recommends a practical approach: taste the food with closed eyes, consider its fat, salt, and acid content, then select a wine that achieves balance. She consults online recipes and researches recommended pairings when uncertain.

## Featured Recipe Pairings

- **Butternut Squash Soup with Latin Twist**: Pairs with Grenache for its berry notes and medium tannins that complement chorizo spice
- **Grilled Steak Tacos**: Traditional Sunday gathering dish
- **Frijoles con Chorizo**: Pairs with Tres Uvas blend (Grenache, Syrah, Petite Sirah) for enhanced tannins that match spicy chorizo
