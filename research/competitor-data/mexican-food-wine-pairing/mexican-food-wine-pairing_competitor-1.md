# Wine to Pair with Mexican Food

**By John H. Isacs | August 8, 2010**

## Overview

While beer is commonly associated with Mexican cuisine, wine actually pairs exceptionally well with authentic Mexican cooking. The article argues that authentic Mexican cuisine is "as diversified and complex as European cuisines" and deserves wine pairings that complement its sophisticated flavors.

## Key Pairing Principles

The author emphasizes that successful food and wine pairing requires deciding whether to match similar qualities or create contrasts between the wine and dish.

## Pairing Recommendations by Ingredient

### Tomato-Based Dishes

For cold dishes and seafood preparations heavy in tomatoes, the author recommends acidic white wines. Specifically, New Zealand Sauvignon Blancs from producers like Forrest Estate work well due to their liveliness and acidity.

For Mexican meat dishes with tomato sauces, medium-bodied Italian reds are suggested, such as Lungarotto Sangiovese or Benti di Batasiolo Barbara d'Asti.

### Chili-Based Dishes

Structured reds with high tannins should be avoided, as they intensify chili's spiciness and overwhelm the palate. Instead, robust white or red wines with fruity characteristics help soothe the palate.

The author recommends California Zinfandels or Australian Shiraz for meat and cheese dishes with chilies, citing examples like Kendall Jackson Vinters Reserve Zinfandel and Wolf Blass Grey Label Shiraz.
