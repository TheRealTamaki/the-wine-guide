# It's Grilling Season: 5 Best Wines for BBQ Foods

Things are heating up at Coravin HQ. The sun is shining, temperatures are climbing, and the team is working to change how the world experiences wine. As a company, they balance hard work with enjoying time collaborating during the day, then drinking wine on restaurant patios or in backyards at night. Weekends bring outdoor grilling sessions and barbecues.

Cookouts using grills (hot flame cooking) or BBQ (indirect smoking) are common warm-weather gatherings in New England, where Coravin is headquartered. This roundup covers the best wines for BBQ foods including grilled chicken, burgers, and smoked ribs.

## 5 Best Wines for BBQ Foods and Grilling

**Quick answer:** When uncertain, choose rosé, which pairs well with almost every grilled or smoked food.

### 1. Sweet BBQ Pork and Zinfandel

Sweet sauce pairs well with sweet wine. For pork smoked with sweet BBQ sauce, honey, maple, or classic varieties, opt for Zinfandel or Riesling. These are great entry-level wines for introducing friends to new beverages.

### 2. Spicy BBQ Steak and Sangiovese

For spicy black pepper BBQ sauce with steak, choose a medium-bodied red like Italian Sangiovese or Spanish Tempranillo. Both feature cherry notes, herb flavors, and medium-high acidity, with high-quality, affordable options widely available.

### 3. Grilled Chicken and Sauvignon Blanc

White meat pairs well with white wine. For classic grilled chicken, Sauvignon Blanc is recommended, or try Vinho Verde for a summertime crowd-pleaser.

### 4. Beef Burgers and Cabernet Sauvignon

Beef burgers call for Cabernet Sauvignon, with Syrah or Shiraz as close alternatives. Cabernet Sauvignon's black fruit flavors combined with notes of cedar, baking spices, and graphite, pair well with grilled red meats and peppery sauces. Choose Cabernet for black peppery burgers or Syrah for spicy versions.

### 5. Veggie Skewers and Chenin Blanc

Vegetables deserve spotlight attention. Top choice for grilled veggies is Chenin Blanc, followed by white Bordeaux. Chenin Blanc offers light body, high acidity, and refreshing fruit with chamomile and honey flavors.

Note: Grilled fish options were intentionally excluded. Sparkling wine suits salmon, while white fish works with grilled chicken pairings.
