# BBQ Season is Here: What Wines Pair with Grilled Favorites?

By Krista Smith. July 22, 2024.

## Introduction

Summer offers the perfect opportunity to gather with loved ones and enjoy grilled cuisine. To elevate your barbecue experience, consider pairing complementary wines with your favorite foods. The following guide suggests wine options that enhance both the meal and reveal different dimensions of the wines themselves.

## Wines to Pair with Grilled Steak

**Red Wines:**
- **Cabernet Sauvignon**: Features dark fruit flavors and spice with strong tannins that balance the richness of steak
- **Zinfandel**: Offers fruity and spicy notes that complement the smoky character of grilled red meat

**White Wine:**
- **Oaked Chardonnay**: Creamy texture with buttery notes that enhance the steak's richness

## Wines to Pair with Grilled Hamburgers

**Red Wines:**
- **Merlot**: Soft tannins with cherry, plum, and chocolate notes work well with savory burgers
- **Syrah/Shiraz**: Peppery kick and dark fruit flavors match the smoky, charred taste

**White Wine:**
- **Sauvignon Blanc**: Crisp acidity and citrus notes cut through meat richness

## Wines to Pair with Grilled Chicken

**Red Wine:**
- **Pinot Noir**: Light-bodied with bright acidity and red berry flavors

**White Wine:**
- **Chenin Blanc**: Apple, pear, and honey notes complement chicken naturally

## Wines to Pair with BBQ Ribs

**Red Wines:**
- **Malbec**: Rich, velvety flavors of blackberries and plums complement smoky, saucy ribs
- **Zinfandel**: Fruity and spicy profile enhances barbecue sauce flavors

**White Wines:**
- **Gewürztraminer**: Floral and spicy notes handle strong barbecue sauce flavors
- **Pinot Gris/Grigio**: Skin-contact versions develop heartier tannins for rich pork dishes

## Wines to Pair with Bratwurst

**Red Wine:**
- **Grenache**: Fruit-forward with red berry and spice notes; low tannins match the sausage

**White Wines:**
- **Riesling**: Off-dry style balances spicy elements with peach and apricot flavors
- **Grenache Blanc**: High acidity with honeydew and green apple notes cuts through spice

## Wines to Pair with Grilled Seafood

**Red Wine:**
- **Pinot Noir**: Light-bodied with red berry flavors and bright acidity

**White Wines:**
- **Pinot Grigio**: Crisp acidity with lime and green apple for lighter fish
- **Albariño**: High acidity and citrus notes work with various fish preparations
- **Chardonnay**: Minerality and bright acidity enhance shellfish sweetness

## Wines to Pair with Grilled Vegetables

**Red Wines:**
- **Rosé**: Bright acidity and strawberry and watermelon flavors enhance vegetable dishes

**White Wines:**
- **Vermentino**: Light and zesty with citrus and herb notes
- **Viognier**: Richer style with tropical or citrus qualities that enhance natural sweetness

## Wines to Pair with Hot Dogs

**Red Wines:**
- **Pinot Noir**: Berry notes with subtle smoky flavors pair well with classic toppings
- **GSM**: Grenache, Syrah, and Mourvèdre blend offering red fruit and peppery notes

**White Wines:**
- **Sauvignon Blanc**: Crisp acidity refreshes the palate between bites

## Conclusion

Summer barbecues benefit from thoughtful wine selections that complement grilled offerings. Explore these pairing suggestions to enhance your next gathering.
