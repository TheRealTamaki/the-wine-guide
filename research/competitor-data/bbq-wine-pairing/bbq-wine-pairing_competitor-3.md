# How to Pair Wine with BBQ

By Natalie Williams. Posted on June 19, 2024.

## Introduction

Summer cookouts reach their full potential when paired with thoughtful wine selections. The article explores how Washington State wines complement various BBQ dishes, demonstrating that the right wine elevates the entire dining experience.

## 1. Riesling

This versatile white wine offers a refreshing hit of acidity that can break up the big flavors of BBQ. Riesling's subtle sweetness harmonizes with BBQ sauces while its acidity provides balance.

**Recommended Pairings:**
- Grilled Shrimp Skewers
- Spicy Ribs
- Grilled Pineapple

## 2. Pinot Gris

A delicate white wine that cuts through rich, fatty BBQ dishes with bright acidity. The wine's fruit-forward character enhances natural sweetness in sauces and glazes.

**Recommended Pairings:**
- Grilled Vegetable Kabobs
- BBQ Chicken
- Grilled Fish Tacos

## 3. Rosé of Syrah

This uncommon wine offers boldness with refreshing qualities, combining crisp acidity and fruit-forward flavors. It works as a pleasant surprise that elevates ordinary dishes.

**Recommended Pairings:**
- Grilled Watermelon
- Pulled Pork Sliders
- Spicy Kielbasa

## 4. Merlot

Known for its smooth, approachable character, Merlot features notes of plum, cherry, and blackberry with hints of cocoa, vanilla, and spice, creating harmony with smoky, savory BBQ flavors.

**Recommended Pairings:**
- BBQ-Glazed Chicken Wings
- Grilled Pork Chops
- Classic Cheeseburgers

## 5. Cabernet Sauvignon

A powerhouse red wine with full-bodied structure and intense flavors. Washington Cabernets feature black currant, blackberry, and dark cherry notes complementing BBQ's bold character.

**Recommended Pairings:**
- Ribeye Steak
- Grilled Lamb Chops
- Grilled Portobello Mushrooms

## 6. Syrah

Syrah's peppery nature harmonizes with BBQ spices while its smoky undertones mirror the grill's charred essence. The wine features blackberry, plum, and blueberry with black pepper and smoked meat notes.

**Recommended Pairings:**
- Smoked Brisket
- Grilled Steaks
- Grilled Eggplant and Zucchini

## Serving Temperature

While tradition suggests serving red wines at room temperature, chilling them enhances refreshment during summer heat. Personal preference ultimately guides this choice.

## Conclusion

Selecting appropriate wine pairings transforms summer cookouts into memorable culinary experiences, whether enjoying shrimp, brisket, or ribeye steak.
