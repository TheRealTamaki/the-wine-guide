# Summer Wine Cocktails To Sip With Brunch

## The Best Wine Cocktails For Summer Brunch

### Mint Peach Rose Spritzer

This refreshing beverage combines "peaches and fresh mint, muddled together and mixed with a crisp Rose and Sparkling wine." The drink benefits from overnight steeping to allow flavors to meld properly.

Ingredients:
- 1 bottle Rose wine
- 1 bottle Sparkling wine
- 4 sprigs fresh mint, plus leaves for garnish
- 3 tablespoons sugar
- 4 large peaches, pitted and thinly sliced

Directions:

Muddle sugar and mint in a large pitcher until fragrant. Add sliced peaches and rose, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours. When serving, slowly incorporate the sparkling wine and add mint garnish.

Recommended Pairings:
- El Bombero Rosado with Eggs Florentine
- Il Casato Rose with Smoked Salmon and Bagel plate
- Covalli Prosecco with Charcuterie Board

### Mango White Wine Slushie

An acidic Sauvignon Blanc works best here because the acid balances out the sweetness of mango fruit.

Ingredients:
- 1 cup Sauvignon Blanc
- 2 cups frozen mango cubes
- 1 cup ice
- Sugar or sweetener (optional)

Directions:

Pulse ice and mango in a blender several times. Add wine and blend until smooth. Adjust sweetness as needed and serve immediately.

Recommended Pairings:
- Fly By Sauvignon Blanc with Avocado Toast and Fried Egg
- William Knuttel Sauvignon Blanc with Yogurt Acai Bowl
- Sebes Estate Sauvignon Blanc with Lemon Berry Ricotta Pancakes

### Sangria Mexican Mule

This reimagined cocktail incorporates red wine into the traditional ginger beer formula.

Ingredients:
- 1 ounce red wine (Pinot Noir or Red Blend suggested)
- 2 ounces tequila
- 1/2 ounce fresh lime juice
- 4 ounces ginger beer
- Blackberries and lime wedges for garnish

Directions:

Fill a shaker with ice, add wine, tequila, and lime juice. Shake and strain over fresh ice in a rocks glass. Top with ginger beer, stir gently, and garnish with blackberries and lime.

Recommended Pairings:
- Big Bad Pinot Noir with Quiche Lorraine
- Tri-Tip Red Blend with Baked Oatmeal and Macerated Fruit
- Las Vides Red Blend with Blueberry French Toast Bake
