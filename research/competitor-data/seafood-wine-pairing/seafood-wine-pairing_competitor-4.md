# Wine & Seafood Pairings: Everything You Should Know

Published May 30, 2025 | Eddie Merlot's

## Why Wine and Seafood Are a Perfect Match

The combination works because seafood offers varied textures and flavors, from delicate white fish to rich, meaty options, while wine's diversity complements these profiles. As the article notes, "The subtle citrus note in a white wine can bring out the natural sweetness in shellfish."

The key principle: quality pairings elevate rather than overpower, and no single formula applies universally.

## How Acidity, Body, and Flavor Come Into Play

### Acidity
Higher-acidity wines like Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Grigio, and sparkling options refresh the palate and cut through richness or brininess, enhancing shellfish sweetness.

### Body
Wine weight should match fish weight. Delicate fish (flounder, tilapia) pair with lighter wines, while heartier varieties (tuna, salmon, swordfish) handle medium-bodied or lighter reds.

### Flavor Profile
Harmony matters most. Bold sauces call for equally bold wines, while raw or subtly seasoned fish demands crisp, clean selections. Spicy seafood benefits from off-dry aromatics.

## Debunking Common Myths

**Red wine with fish:** Light-bodied Pinot Noir works beautifully with oily fish like salmon and tuna, contrary to the "white wine only" myth.

**Sparkling wine limitations:** Bubbles serve as palate cleansers and pair exceptionally well with grilled shrimp, raw oysters, and spicy seafood, far beyond celebratory occasions.

## Best Wine Pairings for Popular Seafood

| Seafood | Wine | Why It Works |
|---------|------|------------|
| Lobster | Chardonnay (lightly oaked) | Buttery texture mirrors the seafood's richness |
| Crab Legs | Sauvignon Blanc | Zesty citrus brightens natural sweetness |
| Scallops | Pinot Grigio | Clean finish won't overpower delicate flavor |
| Salmon | Pinot Noir | Earthy notes complement the fish's richness |
| Shrimp | Sparkling Wine | Effervescence enhances briny flavors elegantly |

## Tips for Choosing the Right Wine

**Consider preparation style:** Rich sauces demand fuller-bodied whites; grilled dishes with char pair better with high-acidity options. Spicy preparations suit off-dry Rieslings or aromatic Roses.

**Match intensity, not just color:** Balance prevents either component from overwhelming the other. Tuna can handle Pinot Noir; delicate flounder needs something lighter.

**Experiment freely:** Wine pairing is creative exploration rather than rigid science. Unexpected combinations often yield delightful discoveries.
