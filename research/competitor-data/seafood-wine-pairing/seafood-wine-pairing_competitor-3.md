# Seafood and Wine Pairing Guide

Here in Australia, we enjoy abundant fresh seafood year-round alongside world-class wines. When these two come together, the result is truly magical.

## What Kind of Wine Goes Well with Seafood?

The style of wine you choose depends on the delicacy of your seafood dish. Several categories work well:

**Light and Aromatic Wines** pair excellently with snapper and Asian-flavored dishes.

**Medium and Textural White Wines** complement blue swimmer crab and grilled seafood preparations.

**Fuller-Bodied White Wines** work beautifully with seafood in creamy sauces.

**Light to Medium-Weight Savoury Reds** suit oily fish or tomato-based seafood dishes perfectly.

## Best Wine to Pair with Oysters

Pairing Sparkling wine with freshly-shucked oysters is a winning combination. Semillons or young Rieslings are also superb choices for this classic pairing.

## Best Wine to Pair with Scallops

Seared scallops pair wonderfully with Australian Sparkling wine. Light aromatic whites including Semillon, Sauvignon Blanc, Semillon and Sauvignon Blanc blends, or Riesling make elegant matches.

## Best Wine for Prawns

Australia's sweet, fresh prawns pair beautifully with Sparkling, Semillon, Sauvignon Blanc, Semillon and Sauvignon Blanc, and Riesling. When combined with Asian flavors, Gruner Veltliner or Gewurztraminer shine. With sauces and meat flavors, chilled Rose works wonderfully.

## What Wine Goes with Ceviche?

Very delicate ceviches need wines with similar qualities. Super-dry Rose, young Semillon, or lighter styles of Vermentino are ideal. Recipes with wasabi kick pair well with Riesling or Gruner Veltliner.

## Best Wine with Crab

Simple and light crab dishes need a white wine that's light on the oak with crisp acidity. Medium-weight and textural options like Marsanne, Pinot Gris, Vermentino, Arneis, and Fiano work beautifully.

## Mussels with Wine

Light, sea-fresh mussels traditionally call for white wine. Refined Riesling pairs perfectly. More powerful Mediterranean preparations need wines with greater intensity, making Grenache Mataro or Shiraz Cabernet excellent choices.

## Wine with Fish

Fried fish loves the natural acidity of Semillon, Sauvignon Blanc, Riesling, and Semillon Sauvignon Blanc blends. Weightier grilled fish can handle medium-weight options like Marsanne, Pinot Gris, Arneis, and Fiano. Vermentino displays classic salty characters ideal for simply-prepared seafood.

## Wine with Salmon

The richness of fish like salmon make it great for red wine-lovers. Light to medium-weight savoury reds like Grenache, GSM Blends, Nero d'Avola, Barbera, Pinot Noir, and Merlot work excellently. Rose offers another delicious choice. These varieties also pair well with rich fish stews, curries, bouillabaisse, and oily fish like tuna and mackerel.

## Best Wine with Calamari

Prosecco-battered calamari pairs perfectly with Prosecco and sparkling whites, plus light aromatic options like Riesling, Semillon, Sauvignon Blanc, Gruner Veltliner, or Gewurztraminer. Brightly flavored dishes benefit from dry Australian Rose like Tempranillo Rose.

## Best Wine with Lobster

Rich, barbequed lobster pairs beautifully with fuller-bodied and richer whites like Chardonnay, Roussanne, Verdelho, or Viognier.
