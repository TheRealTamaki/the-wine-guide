# Wonderful Winter wines for cozy evenings

By Anne Arnold | December 12, 2022

As the season shifts toward colder weather and heartier meals, wine selections should evolve accordingly. Here are several wine styles worth exploring during winter months.

## Go bubbly to start

Champagne: The holiday season presents an ideal opportunity to celebrate with bubbly. For budget-conscious selections, Pommery Brut Royal NV offers the classic blend of Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and Meunier. Premium options include Lanson Black Label and Charles Heidsieck, both showcasing depth from reserve wine components.

Cremant (de Bourgogne, d'Alsace, de Limoux, etc): These traditional method sparkling wines produced outside Champagne offer exceptional value. Cremant de Bourgogne provides an affordable alternative featuring Chardonnay and Pinot Noir bases similar to Champagne.

## Winter Whites

Chardonnay: Cooler months call for oaked, buttery expressions from California, Australia, or Burgundy. Secondary flavors including toast, smoke, vanilla, and spices complement heavier seasonal fare better than lighter options.

Chenin Blanc: This South African workhorse grape also thrives in France's Loire region. Offering more substance than Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio, Chenin Blanc represents an ideal winter white selection.

## Heartier reds with character

Cotes du Rhone Villages and Rhone blends: These Grenache-Syrah-Mourvedre combinations from southern France pair beautifully with stews, braised meats, and comfort foods. They balance character with approachable tannins.

California and West Coast red blends: Cabernet-dominant Meritage blends showcase winemaking artistry. Buena Vista Winery's Sheriff represents a local favorite.

Nebbiolos from Piemonte: Whether selecting Gattinara, Barolo, or Barbaresco, these food-friendly Italian reds deliver substance. Their pale color belies rich tannins and crisp acidity.

## After dinner: Fortified Wines

Port: These Portuguese gems range across styles and price points. Late Bottled Vintage pairs elegantly with dark chocolate.

Tawny Port: Golden-hued alternatives to ruby ports offer toasty, nutty, spiced character suitable for finishing meals and pairing with desserts.

Sherry: Amontillado and Oloroso expressions provide rich alternatives to whiskey for cold-weather evenings.

The recommendation: embrace wines with more substance, spice, and tannin to enhance winter enjoyment.
