# Best Indian Wines to Warm Your Winters

By Kriti Malhotra | Dec-Jan 2020

## Introduction

India's wine heritage stretches back centuries. The history of wine in India goes back to the Maurya Dynasty, more than 300 years BC, when Emperor Chandragupta Maurya savored both local white and red wines. Though wine culture faded as India became known for brown spirits, contemporary consumers, particularly millennials, are rediscovering these beverages.

This winter season offers an opportunity to explore premium Indian red wines that deserve recognition for their quality and value.

Serving tip: Use large-bowled red wine glasses for optimal enjoyment.

## Featured Indian Red Wines

### Grover Zampa Chene Grande Reserve

This international award-winning blend combines Spanish Tempranillo and French Shiraz grapes, aged 15 months in French oak. The wine showcases spicy characteristics with dark chocolate, licorice, and roasted coffee notes, finishing with blackberry and dark plum flavors.

Decanting suggestion: Minimum 30 minutes

### Fratelli Sette

Created by seven Italian brothers, this wine blends Italian Sangiovese with French Cabernet Sauvignon. It features vanilla aromatics and dark berry intensity, matured in French oak. The complex profile appeals to experienced wine drinkers familiar with Tuscan styles.

Decanting suggestion: 50 minutes (extendable to 90 minutes)

### Reveilo Nero d'Avola

This Sicilian grape thrives in Nashik's similar climate. The wine presents cherry and strawberry aromas with black pepper and clove spice notes. It is approachable, straightforward, and enjoyable either solo or alongside food.

### York Arros

A Nashik-produced blend of Shiraz and Cabernet Sauvignon aged in French and American oak, this wine balances complexity with approachability. Expect jammy vanilla, Christmas cake characteristics, and spice notes of nutmeg and cloves, ideal as an aperitif.

### Krsma Cabernet Sauvignon

From a boutique Tungabhadra valley winery near Hampi, Karnataka, this bold yet subtle wine displays layered complexity with blackberry, dark cherry, leather, chocolate, and oak characteristics. Currently available only in Bengaluru and Hyderabad.

Decanting suggestion: Minimum 50 minutes

### Sula Rasa Shiraz

Among India's premium reserves, this bold Shiraz rests 12 months in French oak barrels. It exhibits white pepper aromatics alongside smoky, oaky, and vegetal notes, pairing excellently with meats and cheeses.

Decanting suggestion: Minimum 30 minutes
