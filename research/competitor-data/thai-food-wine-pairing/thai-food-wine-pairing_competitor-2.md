# Best Wine to Pair with Thai Food

October 24th, 2009 | By IntoWine Staff

## Overview

Thai cuisine features robust spices that pair well with crisp, bright wines that complement and enhance its complex flavors. Wine experts recommend aromatic white varieties with lower alcohol content.

## Expert Recommendations

### Aromatic Whites & Sparklers

According to Cezar Kusik, Fine Wine Specialist at Fourcade & Hecht Wine Selections, aromatic wines like Riesling, Gewurztraminer, and Pinot Gris are obvious pairings. He suggests choosing wines with alcohol levels below 12%, as higher alcohol clashes with spicy foods. Germany and Austria produce excellent options, as do lighter Alsatian varieties.

Kusik also recommends Italian sparklers, particularly Asti Spumante from Piedmont or Prosecco from Veneto, noting that "the bubbles seem to enhance the culinary sensation and add flair."

### German Spatlese Rieslings

Tony Connell, Personal Wine Consultant for Montesquieu Wines, favors German Spatlese wines. He notes their "kiss of sweetness" pairs ideally with Thai heat. His favorites include the 2007 JJ Prum Wehlener Sonnenuhr Riesling and 2007 Donnhoff Norheimer Kirschheck Riesling.

### Riesling Focus

Loren Sonkin, Founder/Winemaker at Sonkin Cellars, recommends Riesling for its complexity and versatility across styles and regions. He highlights the basic Donnhoff Riesling bottling (around $20), praising its "grapefruit notes" and thirst-quenching quality when served around 50°F.

### Kerner Alternative

Ben Spencer, a Wine & Spirit Education Trust diploma student, suggests Kerner, a German white wine with citrus and tree fruit notes. He recommends Abbazia di Novacella from Italy's Alto-Adige region, describing it as exceptional with spicy foods.
