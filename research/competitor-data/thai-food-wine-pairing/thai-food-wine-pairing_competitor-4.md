# Best Wines to Drink With Thai Food

Thai cuisine balances sweet, sour, salty, and spicy notes, making wine pairing essential for enhancing the dining experience. According to the article, "bold red wines with heavy tannins often clash with Thai spices," so lighter, aromatic wines work better.

## Top 6 Wine Pairings

**1. Riesling**
An off-dry Riesling pairs excellently with Thai curries and noodle dishes. Its acidity cuts through coconut-based sauces while subtle sweetness counters heat.

**2. Gewürztraminer**
This aromatic white offers floral and lychee notes, complementing spicy stir-fries and tom yum soup particularly well.

**3. Sauvignon Blanc**
Crisp and zesty, this wine matches dishes highlighting fresh ingredients like lemongrass and herbs, such as spring rolls and basil stir-fries.

**4. Rosé**
A versatile, refreshing choice that works with mixed appetizers, grilled meats, and Thai barbecue when sharing multiple dishes.

**5. Sparkling Wine**
Prosecco, Cava, or Champagne cleanse the palate between bites and complement fried appetizers and spicy noodle dishes.

**6. Pinot Noir**
Light-bodied reds with soft tannins pair well with grilled chicken, satay, and roasted duck curry without overpowering delicate flavors.

## Pairing Tips

- Choose wines with slight sweetness to balance chili heat
- Prioritize high acidity to refresh your palate
- Avoid heavy, tannic reds that clash with fish sauce and spices
- Match wine intensity to the dish's spice level
