# Pairing Wine With Pasta - Six Classic Matches

Published: 06 November 2023
Written by: Nikki Jacoby

## Overview

Pasta offers remarkable versatility for wine pairing. The article explains that success comes from understanding key principles rather than rigid rules.

## 4 Principles for Pairing Wine with Pasta

1. **Don't restrict yourself to regional pairings** — While Italian wines naturally complement pasta, quality options exist worldwide.

2. **Focus on the sauce** — The sauce's characteristics matter more than pasta shape. Consider the meats, vegetables, and flavor profiles present.

3. **Reds suit tomato-based sauces** — High-tannin, acidic reds cut through fatty meats and complement rich tomato flavors.

4. **Whites work with cream and cheese sauces** — Oaky Chardonnay or crisp Pinot Grigio cuts through richness effectively.

## Six Classic Pairings

### Spaghetti Bolognese
**Recommended wines:** Chianti, Primitivo, Malbec

Medium to high tannin reds balance the fatty meat and acidic tomato sauce.

### Mac and Cheese
**Recommended wines:** Chardonnay, Riesling

Light unoaked Chardonnay's acidity cuts through creamy richness, while off-dry Riesling balances sweetness with the dish's saltiness.

### Puttanesca
**Recommended wines:** Riesling, Barbera, Zinfandel

Crisp whites or light, fruity reds with low-to-medium tannins work best, as high-tannin wines clash with spicy elements.

### Carbonara
**Recommended wines:** Chardonnay, Chenin Blanc

Well-structured whites complement the egg's slight sweetness while providing needed crispness.

### Lasagne
**Recommended wines:** Chianti, Shiraz

Medium-to-high acidity reds with fruity and herbal notes complement the rich meat sauce and creamy béchamel layers.

### Seafood Pasta
**Recommended wines:** Pinot Grigio, Vinho Verde

Italian whites with minerality and acidity enhance delicate seafood flavors, while Vinho Verde offers a fresh, slightly fizzy alternative.
