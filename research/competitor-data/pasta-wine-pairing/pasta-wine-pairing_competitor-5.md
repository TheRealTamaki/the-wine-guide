# Pasta and Wine: Perfect Pairings

## If you want to eat like an Italian, you should drink like them too!

Pasta and wine have been paired together for a long time. They're some of the oldest pleasures in history, making them natural companions at the table.

## What kind of wine goes with pasta?

The simplest answer is that all wine pairs well with pasta, especially if it's your favorite wine. The key is finding combinations that appeal to your palate.

More nuanced pairings depend on the specific pasta dish:

- **Creamy sauces** (like alfredo): Light-bodied white wines such as Chardonnay
- **Olive oil-based dishes** (Aglio e Olio, Carbonara): Crisp dry whites like Sauvignon Blanc
- **Pesto pasta**: Rustic Sauvignon Blanc
- **Tomato-based sauces**: Medium-bodied wines like Zinfandel to balance acidity
- **Spicy sauces**: Sharp, dry wines for contrasting flavors

**Consider the accompanying ingredients:**

- Tomato sauce with rich meats or cream: Full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon
- Seafood pastas: Refreshing Pinot Grigio or Prosecco
- Vegetarian dishes: Light wines with citrus notes

## Do you drink red or white wine with pasta?

Both red and white wines work with pasta dishes. Fresh tomato sauce pairs well with dry whites, while meat-based tomato dishes complement tart, medium-bodied reds. Rosé offers a middle ground.

## What drinks go with pasta?

Beyond wine, other beverages enhance pasta meals:

- **Seafood pasta**: Sparkling water with lemon
- **Pesto or vegetable dishes**: Paloma cocktail or mocktail
- **Spicy pasta**: Thai iced tea
- **Rich, creamy pasta**: Italian Shakerato (iced espresso) or berry Italian soda

## What red wine goes with Spaghetti?

Pinot Noir is ideal for classic spaghetti, offering lighter structure and notes of rose, mushroom, cherry and hibiscus that enhance traditional Italian flavors.

---

**Remember:** Personal preference matters most. Experiment with different combinations to discover your own perfect pairings.
