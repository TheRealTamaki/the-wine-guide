# Pairing Wine with Pasta

From quick mid-week carbonara to traditional bolognese, pasta dishes deserve thoughtful wine pairings. This guide explores which wines complement different pasta styles.

## Tomato-Based Pasta Dishes

Medium-bodied Italian red wines work best with tomato sauces. The recommended varieties include Nebbiolo, Barbera, and Nero d'Avola. For lighter vegetable-forward dishes with sweeter elements like pumpkin, textural white wines such as Fiano offer excellent alternatives.

### Featured Recipes

**Paccheri Genovese** pairs well with Nebbiolo, a wine that sits between big, tannic reds and lighter Pinot Noir styles. Its acidity and tannins balance high-fat pork and beef dishes effectively.

**Rainbow Chard and Ricotta Cannelloni** complements Barbera, which features natural acidity and lower tannins than most Italian varieties, creating juicy black fruit flavors ideal for tomato-based sauces.

**Pappardelle with Duck Ragu and Porcini** benefits from Nero d'Avola's fragrant, crunchy character: light to medium-bodied with Pinot-like qualities.

## Creamy and Cheesy Pasta

Rich, indulgent cream and cheese dishes require wines with good natural acidity to offset their heaviness. Suitable options include Sauvignon Blanc, Fiano, Vermentino, and Chardonnay.

**Goat's Cheese Pasta** pairs naturally with Sauvignon Blanc's herbal notes and dry, crisp profile.

**Blue Cheese Gnocchi** works with Vermentino's light-to-medium body, fresh character, and almost salty complexity.

**Calamari Carbonara** suits unoaked Chardonnay, offering gentle citrus and herb notes that complement the dish's similar flavors.

## Vegetable Pasta Dishes

Wine selection depends on specific vegetables and sauces, ranging from fresh, textural whites to light-to-medium reds.

**Pesto Pasta** pairs with refreshing Pinot Grigio.

**Mushroom and Garlic Orecchiette** complements Pinot Noir's bright acidity, red fruit flavors, and earthy depth, particularly effective with umami-rich mushrooms.

**Sweet Potato Gnocchi** benefits from light-oak white wines with crisp acidity like Marsanne, Pinot Grigio, Vermentino, Arneis, and Fiano.

**Open Lasagne** suits Grenache's mid-weight silken character, ideal for mushroom-based meals.

## Seafood Pasta

Standard seafood-wine pairing principles apply to seafood pasta recipes.

**Prawn, Cherry Tomato, and Saffron Pasta** pairs with Pinot Grigio's refreshing, juicy, textural profile.

**Blue Swimmer Crab Spaghettini** matches Riesling's high acidity and citrussy character perfectly. Crab and Riesling are classic partners.

**Malloreddus with Vongole** works with Vermentino, featuring stone fruit, citrus, dried herbs, and signature saline character.

**Prawn and Eggplant Pasta** suits Nero d'Avola, which offers fragrant, light-to-medium body qualities with medium tannins and higher acidity needed for this rich tomato-based dish.
