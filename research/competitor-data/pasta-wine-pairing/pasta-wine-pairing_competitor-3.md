# The Best Wine with Pasta: 7 Great Options

## The 7 BEST Wines for Pasta

1. Cabernet Sauvignon
2. Pinot Noir
3. Zinfandel
4. Merlot
5. Syrah
6. Sauvignon Blanc
7. Chardonnay

## Does Pasta Go with Red or White Wine?

The selection depends on the sauce style. Tomato-based sauces pair best with acidic red wines, while creamy or seafood preparations work better with white varieties.

## Red Wine and Pasta Sauce

For marinara-based dishes, consider Zinfandel, Merlot, or Syrah. These wines feature sufficient acidity to complement high-acid tomato sauces. Without proper acidity balance, the pairing can taste unpleasant.

## White Wine Options for Pasta

Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, and Riesling are excellent selections for lighter pasta preparations.

## Creamy Pasta Pairings

Dishes like Alfredo, Cheese Manicotti, or Goat Cheese Pasta pair well with light-bodied options including Chardonnay or lighter reds like Pinot Noir.

## Mushroom Pasta

Earthy red wines such as Pinot Noir complement mushroom-based dishes nicely.

## Meat-Based Pastas

Heavier preparations like Sunday Gravy, Lasagna, or Bolognese benefit from full-bodied selections such as Cabernet Sauvignon and Syrah.

## Seafood Pastas

Lobster Ravioli, Shrimp Fra Diavolo, and Squid Ink Pasta pair well with unoaked Chardonnay or Sauvignon Blanc.

## Pesto Dishes

A ripe, full-bodied Chardonnay complements pesto preparations, as its creaminess is a great match for the bright flavors of fresh pesto.

## Light Olive Oil-Based Pastas

Crisp, dry Sauvignon Blanc suits dishes like Aglio e Olio or Lemon Pasta.

## Final Thoughts

Wine pairing preferences are subjective. The article encourages experimentation with different varieties to find personal favorites, including sparkling wine options.
