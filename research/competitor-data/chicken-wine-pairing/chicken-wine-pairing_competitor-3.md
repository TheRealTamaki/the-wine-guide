# Chicken Wine Pairing

## Best Wines to Pair with Chicken

Chicken can be cooked many ways, and many wines can go with it. Familiar chicken variations include coq au vin, chicken cacciatore, chicken with cream sauce, roast chicken, grilled chicken, and chicken with herbs.

### What wine to serve with coq au vin

Chicken simmered in red wine with bacon, pearl onions, mushrooms, and garlic works with red Pinot Noir from France, California, Oregon, or other regions that make soft, silky, earthy, spicy Pinot Noir.

For coq au vin blanc, dry white wine with stony earthiness can work better than weighty fruit, sharp Sauvignon Blanc, Riesling, or Moscato. White Burgundy from Macon and Cote de Beaune, Pinot Blanc, and Pinot d'Alsace can suit the dish. Some cooler-climate Chardonnays from Santa Barbara, Oregon, and Washington can also work.

### Pairing chicken cacciatore with wine

Chicken cacciatore is often cooked with tomatoes, herbs, white wine, black olives, and anchovy. Chianti Classico and Brunello di Montalcino, made from Sangiovese, have natural acidity and cherry tomato-like fruitiness that can balance this style of chicken.

### Chicken with cream sauce

Chicken in cream sauce needs wine with enough body for the sauce and enough freshness to keep the dish from feeling heavy. Chardonnay and white Burgundy are useful pairings, especially when the dish includes mushrooms, butter, or herbs.

### Roast chicken

Roast chicken can pair with Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, white Burgundy, and light to medium-bodied red wine. The wine should match the intensity of the seasoning and side dishes.

### Grilled chicken

Grilled chicken can work with Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Grenache, and rose. Herbs, lemon, garlic, smoke, and char influence the final wine choice. Lighter wines work with simple grilled chicken, while more flavorful grilled preparations can handle medium-bodied reds.

### Spicy chicken

Spicy chicken can work with wines that have fruit, acidity, and sometimes residual sugar. Riesling, Gewurztraminer, rose, and aromatic white wines can be useful choices because sweetness and freshness balance heat.

### Barbecue chicken

Barbecue chicken has smoke, sweetness, spice, and sauce. Zinfandel, Grenache, Shiraz, and fuller rose can work because fruit helps meet sweet and smoky flavors.
