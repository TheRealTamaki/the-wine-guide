# The Best Wine to Pair with Chicken

Chicken is versatile and pairs with many seasonings, side dishes, and accompaniments. It is economical and flexible with wine. The meat is a hybrid of lean white meat and richer dark meat. A simple roasted chicken can pair with full-flavored red wine, rose, or dry white wine.

The basic pairing principle is to avoid overpowering the meat or dish. Wines with fruit and decent acidity can match richer dishes, but too much tannin can mask chicken flavors.

## Chicken Paired with White Wine

### Champagne and fried chicken

Fried chicken is salty, juicy, crispy, and rich. Champagne works because acidity and effervescence cut through the fried coating. Citrus notes and freshness add lift to the chicken.

### Chardonnay and creamy chicken dishes

Chardonnay is known for a buttery, smooth taste and a creamy mouthfeel while keeping citrus tones. It pairs with creamy chicken dishes such as fettuccine Alfredo and chicken pot pie. Chablis, Napa Chardonnay, Sonoma Coast Chardonnay, and Carneros Chardonnay are examples of styles that can work.

### Sauvignon Blanc and lemon, garlic, herb chicken

Sauvignon Blanc has citrus overtones that match lemony chicken dishes with garlic or herbs. It is light enough for delicate herbal rubs and has a crisp finish that keeps chicken tasting fresh.

### Spicy chicken dishes

Heat likes sweet. Aromatic white wines such as medium-dry Riesling, Pinot Gris, and fruity rose can complement spicy chicken dishes.

### Dry white wine with artichokes or asparagus

Chicken dishes with artichokes or asparagus can be difficult with wine. Dry white wines with high acidity and little residual sugar can help. Champagne, Sauvignon Blanc, Gruner Veltliner, and Albarino are useful options.

## Chicken Paired with Red or White Wine

### Roasted chicken

Simple roasted chicken should be paired with wines that do not overwhelm the food. It can pair with full-flavored red wine, medium-bodied rose, or dry white wine. Oaked Chardonnay and Pinot Noir both work. If the roast chicken has dark savory gravy, medium-bodied red wine such as Cotes du Rhone can work.

## Chicken Paired with Red or Rose

### Chicken with savory or earthy flavors

Red wine is best with chicken when the preparation includes earthy ingredients such as mushrooms, root vegetables, tomato sauce, or red wine sauce.

Chicken with tomato and pepper sauces can work with medium-bodied French or Spanish red wine or Merlot. Chicken with barbecue sauce can take fuller-bodied red wine with a touch of sweetness, such as Shiraz, Grenache, or Zinfandel, but the wine should not be too big or oaky.

Coq au vin cooked with dry Burgundy, shallots, garlic, and mushrooms can work with Merlot. Merlot has fewer bitter tannins than Cabernet Sauvignon, with black cherry notes that can complement garlic and savory complexity. Medium-bodied rose is versatile with bigger flavors such as anchovy, olives, garlic, saffron, and pimenton.
