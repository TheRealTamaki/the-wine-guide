# Wine & Cheese Pairing Guide

We've all found ourselves wandering the cheese aisle, unsure which selection pairs well with wine. This guide outlines optimal cheese pairings for Wolfe Heights wines.

## White Wine

**Albariño:** Soft cheeses like burrata, or semi-hard varieties such as manchego, gouda, and salty feta complement this wine.

**Roussanne:** Pairs well with mild cheddar, Gouda, Smoked Gouda, Monterey Jack, Triple Creme, Spanish sheep's milk cheese, and Brie.

**Chardonnay:** Select pungent or high-acid cheeses such as blue cheese, or nutty cheddar with parmesan-like consistency. Steer clear of funky, washed-rind varieties (taleggio, Époisse), and aged goat and sheep's cheese.

## Rosé Wine

**Grenache Rosé:** Young cheeses including feta, mozzarella, cheddar, and baby swiss work best.

**Rosé Blend:** Try salty, fresh cheeses like Feta or Halloumi; or creamy fresh cheese like Burrata.

## Red Wines

**Merlot:** Pairs nicely with gouda, gorgonzola, brie, Jarlsberg or parmesan.

**Sangiovese:** Aged Tuscan Pecorino, blue cheese, Grana Padano Stravecchio, and aged Asiago complement this wine's acidity and flavor.

**Syrah:** Gravitate toward harder or pungent cheeses. Smoked Gouda is excellent. Bleu cheeses like Gorgonzola or Stilton, and hard varieties like Parmesan or asiago also pair well.

**Petite Sirah:** Consider Camembert, Swiss, Mozzarella, or Gouda. Avoid aged cheeses.

**Malbec:** Pair with pungent, soft cheeses like gorgonzola or blue cheese. For milder preferences, try Monterey Jack, Provolone, and melted Swiss cheese.

**Tempranillo:** Pairs well with sheep's milk cheeses.

**Cabernet Sauvignon:** Select aged cheddar, gorgonzola, or gouda. Avoid soft cheeses.
