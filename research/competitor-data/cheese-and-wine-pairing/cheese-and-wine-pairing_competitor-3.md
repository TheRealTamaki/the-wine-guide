# 4 Essential Red Wine and Cheese Pairings

Wine and cheese enhance each other when paired thoughtfully. The author presents four successful combinations tested through personal tasting.

## Pairing Principles

The article outlines foundational guidelines:

1. Match flavor intensity between wine and food to create balance
2. Salty foods like cheese amplify wine's fruity characteristics
3. Align flavor components (herbaceous wine with herbaceous cheese)
4. Apply the regional concept: "what grows together, goes together"

The author emphasizes that personal enjoyment supersedes strict rules.

## The Four Pairings

**Chianti Rufina DOCG + Parmigiano Reggiano**

This Tuscan pairing follows the regional principle. The 2011 Chianti Rufina features "aromas of dried cherries, strawberry jam, currants, dried rose petals" with balanced acidity. Aged Parmigiano Reggiano's saltiness and texture complement the wine's tannins and red fruit flavors.

**Napa Valley Red Blend + White Cheddar**

The 2017 Prince and Pedro blend combines Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, and other varieties into a jammy, fruit-forward wine. Semi-soft white cheddar's sharpness amplifies the wine's blackberry characteristics while smoothing tannins.

**Châteauneuf-du-Pape + Gouda**

This Southern Rhône wine presents complex flavors of "black cherry, plums, vanilla, black pepper, violets, Mediterranean herbs." Aged gouda's boldness cuts through the wine's intensity, revealing floral and fruity notes.

**Bordeaux + Camembert**

The unconventional final pairing combines a Merlot-Malbec blend with creamy Camembert. The cheese's umami qualities intensify the wine's herbaceous and cherry notes without overwhelming either component.
