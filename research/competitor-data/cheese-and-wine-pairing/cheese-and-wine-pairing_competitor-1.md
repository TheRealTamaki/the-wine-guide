# Pairing Wines with Cheese: Myth and Reality

## The Great Classic Wine and Cheese Pairings

Wine and cheese combinations have been carefully developed in French cuisine for centuries, aiming to create harmony between flavors. Several traditional pairings exemplify this approach:

- **Brie and Champagne**: The bubbles cleanse the palate while the wine's minerality enhances the cheese's delicate mushroom notes, creating a rich and complex experience.
- **Goat Cheese and Sauvignon Blanc**: The wine's fresh character balances the cheese's saltiness, whether the cheese is young or aged.
- **Blue Cheese and Sweet Wine**: Roquefort paired with Sauternes demonstrates how shared mold characteristics (penicillium in cheese, botrytis in grapes) create sublime flavor harmony.

## Debunking the Red Wine Myth

A common misconception suggests red wine pairs universally with cheese. However, tannins can interact negatively with milk proteins, creating bitterness. The article notes that "light, soft and fruity reds" work best with aged firm cheeses, while full-bodied reds complement washed-rind varieties.

Successful pairings require understanding flavor textures and intensities. Examples include Chardonnay with Comté cheese, where acidity balances richness, and Brouilly with Saint-Marcellin cheese.

## Modern and Audacious Pairings

Contemporary options include:

- Rosé with Crottin de chèvre goat cheese
- Orange wine with aged Tomme
- Mellow wines like Sauternes with blue cheeses

## Hosting a Successful Wine and Cheese Party

Key considerations include:

- Present cheeses in order of flavor intensity, from mild to strong
- Maintain proper serving temperatures
- Use quality accessories for elegant presentation
- Offer varied breads and accompaniments
- Allow time for appreciative tasting
