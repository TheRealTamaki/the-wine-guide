# Paris Wine & Cheese Class Champions Pairings

## Overview

At Paroles de Fromagers, a Parisian cheese shop, instructor Romain Ricciardi challenges conventional wisdom about cheese and wine pairings. Rather than the traditional red wine approach favored in France, he advocates for white wine as often being the superior choice.

## How to Judge a Pairing

Ricciardi outlines a specific methodology:

1. Observe the wine's color, aroma notes (smoky, fruity, minerally, floral)
2. Examine the cheese's appearance and smell
3. Most importantly: "take a bite of cheese, swallow it, take a sip of wine, swallow it, then inhale through your mouth, and exhale through your nose"

This technique prevents the cheese from interfering with wine appreciation.

## Seven Cheese & Wine Pairings

**Goat Cheese & Sauvignon Blanc**
- Selles-sur-Cher (Loire Valley) paired with Beaulieu Frères Pouilly Fumé
- The pairing created balance, with the wine becoming less acidic

**Tomme Corsoise & Sauvignon Blanc**
- Unpasteurized sheep's milk cheese aged 3 months
- "Acidity and fat are always a good combination"

**Comté & Sauvignon Blanc**
- 18-month aged, made in spring
- Buttery, nutty character with crunchy crystals

**Brie de Meaux & Sauvignon Blanc**
- Soft, bloomy rind with mushroom and cauliflower aromas
- Light reds acceptable; heavy reds problematic

**Morbier & Bordeaux**
- Rich, grassy, spicy with distinctive charcoal stripe
- Introduced the principle: "Opposites Attract"

**Epoisses & Bordeaux**
- Gooey, salty, intense with cow aroma
- Washed with salted water and alcohol

**Bleu d'Auvergne & Viognier**
- Cows' milk blue cheese paired with fruity Viognier
- Fruity wine counteracted the strong, salty cheese

## Key Pairing Principles

The instructor emphasizes that "Opposites Attract":
- Fat cheese pairs with acidic wine
- Bitter cheese works with fruity wine
- Salty cheese (like Roquefort) suits sweet white wine

Additionally, cheeses from the same region as wine typically pair well together.
