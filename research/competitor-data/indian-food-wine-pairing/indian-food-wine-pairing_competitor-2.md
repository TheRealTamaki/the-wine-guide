# Wine Pairings for Indian Food, Revisited

Published: Jul 7, 2012 · Modified: Mar 14, 2015

## Overview

This article revisits wine pairing recommendations for Indian cuisine, building on the author's earlier popular post on the subject. The author shares updated insights from recent experimentation with various wines served alongside Indian dishes.

## Red Wine Experiment

The author tested the 2008 Full Moon Red Wine, a red blend from Paso Robles. Despite initial optimism about its spiced profile, the pairing proved problematic. The wine's spice "competed with the spice from the food" rather than complementing it, intensifying heat in the mouth instead of cleansing the palate. The author concluded that while serviceable on its own, this wine was unsuitable for Indian cuisine.

## Successful White Wine Pairings

### Vidal Blanc
The 2010 Greenvale Vidal Blanc ($15) impressed the author. This medium-bodied white features "sweet and tart elements" with a slightly syrupy texture that paired well with chicken tikka masala. As a Rhode Island producer, Greenvale also earned praise for their Chardonnay and Cabernet Franc.

### Gruner Veltliner
The 2011 Ried Sandgrube Gruner Veltliner proved successful alongside chicken tikka masala and vegetable curry. Its minerality and peach notes effectively balanced the food's heat.

## Conclusion

The author remains committed to white wine recommendations for Indian food, though remaining open to future red wine discoveries.
