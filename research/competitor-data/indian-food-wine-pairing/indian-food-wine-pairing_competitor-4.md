# A Flavourful Affair: The Art of Pairing Indian Food with Wine

27/09/2023

## Introduction

Indian cuisine, with its diverse flavors and aromatic spices, presents an exciting opportunity for wine pairing exploration. Nikita Hemani, a WSET Level 2 Award in Wines graduate, challenges conventional thinking about food and wine pairings by moving beyond traditional European dishes.

## Understanding Indian Cuisine

India's vast population and geography create culinary diversity spanning from fragrant northern curries to fiery southern specialties. A common misconception exists that Indian food is dominated by spicy heat, but the reality reveals "a masterful use of aromatic spices, from cumin to cardamom and coriander to cinnamon."

## Wine Pairing Principles

When matching wines with Indian dishes, three key characteristics guide selection:

- Acidity: Cleanses the palate and cuts through richness
- Sweetness: Counters spiciness and enhances aromas
- Alcohol level: Higher alcohol can amplify chili heat perception

## Recommended Wine Pairings

### Sparkling Wine: Prosecco 'Dry' DOCG

The effervescence provides refreshing contrast to fried Indian snacks and chaats, cleansing the palate effectively.

Pairs with: Samosas, pakoras, bhel puri, aloo tikis, papdi chaat

### White Wine: Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc

Known for high acidity and tropical fruit notes, this wine complements herb-based and spinach dishes while enhancing spice complexity.

Pairs with: Saag paneer, palak chaat

### Red Wine: Spanish Garnacha

Red fruit aromas with subtle peppery notes harmonize well with tomato-based curries through lower tannins and fruity balance.

Pairs with: Chicken tikka masala, malai kofta, aloo gobi, vegetable korma

## Conclusion

Pairing Indian cuisine with wine breaks traditional rules and creates balanced dining experiences by exploring aromatic spices and wine characteristics thoughtfully.
