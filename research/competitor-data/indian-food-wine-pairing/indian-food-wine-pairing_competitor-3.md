# Best Wine to Pair with Indian Food

Indian cuisine's bold flavors, aromatic spices, and varied textures create excellent opportunities for wine pairing. The guide recommends selecting wines with specific characteristics to complement these dishes.

## General Pairing Principles

"Off-dry whites like Riesling or Gewürztraminer and fruity reds such as Pinot Noir or Gamay complement the complexity and heat" of Indian dishes.

## Specific Dish Pairings

**Chicken Curry**
An off-dry Riesling works well due to its sweetness and bright acidity, which help balance heat and enhance complex flavors. For red wine preferences, light-bodied Pinot Noir with soft tannins and berry notes pairs nicely without overwhelming the dish.

**Lamb Rogan Josh**
Shiraz brings peppery undertones and dark fruit that match the intensity of this hearty dish. Those preferring white wine might choose Gewürztraminer, which offers floral and lychee notes that provide refreshing contrast.

**Vegetable Samosas**
Zesty Sauvignon Blanc's citrusy acidity cuts through the fried exterior and lifts aromatic spices. Gamay provides a lighter red option with juicy fruit flavors suited to the snack's savory and spicy profile.

**Butter Chicken**
A soft, off-dry Chenin Blanc with stone fruit notes and subtle sweetness complements the tomato-based sauce. Medium-bodied Grenache offers gentle tannins and ripe fruit flavors for red wine drinkers.

**Chana Masala**
Pinot Gris offers crisp acidity and subtle stone fruit notes that enhance this chickpea stew. Alternatively, fruity Zinfandel provides enough body and spice-friendly character for the robust, tangy sauce.

## Key Pairing Tips

- Embrace sweetness: Off-dry wines balance heat and complement spices
- Focus on acidity: High-acidity wines enhance flavors and cut through creamy sauces
- Consider spice level: Choose fruit-forward wines with soft tannins for spicier preparations
