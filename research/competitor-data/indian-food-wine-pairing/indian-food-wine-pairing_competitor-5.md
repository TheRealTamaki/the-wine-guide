# Yes, You Can and Should Pair Wine with Indian Food

by JJ Buckley Fine Wines | Aug 11 2020

## Overview

Indian cuisine presents unique pairing challenges due to its complexity and diverse spice profiles. However, strategic wine selection can enhance rather than clash with these bold flavors.

## Tips on How to Pair Wine and Indian Food

### What's the Sauce?

The sauce foundation determines wine compatibility:

- Tomato-based sauces: White wines with good acidity or fruity rosé
- Cream-based sauces: Deeper reds with medium tannins

### How Spicy Is the Dish?

Counterbalance heat with wines that are lower in alcohol, slightly sweet, and served cool. Mild dishes work with dry wines; medium-heat dishes pair well with off-dry options.

### What's the Protein?

- Meat curries: Full-bodied Pinot Noir or delicate Shiraz
- Chicken, seafood, vegetable curries: Pinot Grigio or Gewürztraminer for acidity and fruitiness

## Specific Dish Pairings

**Vindaloo and Rosé**
Spicy vindaloo sauce matches beautifully with sweet rosés, or light-to-medium reds like Gamay.

**Tikka Masala and Riesling**
The creamy tomato sauce pairs excellently with "fruity, bright Riesling that balances richness" alongside Pinot Grigio or Gewürztraminer.

**Saag Paneer and Sauvignon Blanc**
High acidity cuts through cheese richness. Riesling, lighter Cabernet Sauvignon, or rosé also work.

**Dal-Chawal and Chenin Blanc**
Chenin Blanc's high acidity and light flavor complement the buttery lentil dish.

**Sambar Rice and Shiraz**
Smoky, earthy dishes need stronger wines like Shiraz to enhance spiciness.

**Tandoori Chicken and Pinot Noir**
Contrary to meat-color matching conventions, this roasted dish pairs beautifully with Pinot Noir, Cabernet Sauvignon, Zinfandel, or Riesling.
