## Structural Patterns Across Top 10

1. **Two dominant structural models on this SERP:**
   - Model A: Named-bottle listicles, variety-bucketed (White / Red / Sparkling) — Guardian, 8wines
   - Model B: Dish-pairing guides, one wine per dish — Wine Enthusiast, Wine Folly
   - Model C: Gift guides with use-case labels — VinePair, Robb Report
   - Our opportunity: **hybrid of A + gift-guide labels + the occasion/course filter nobody has built**

2. **FAQ density is zero across all 7 competitors.** Nobody has a FAQ section. This is a live gap — PAA questions are unanswered on-page by any current ranker.

3. **No competitor has a cross-occasion filter** (e.g. "for Christmas dinner / for Christmas Eve aperitif / for Christmas gifts / for the leftovers"). The Guardian gestures at leftovers in prose; nobody has made it navigable.

4. **Sweetness and body scales are absent.** Zero competitors use a per-wine sweetness indicator, body scale, or tannin rating. Our existing WineQuiz + SuitabilityStrip infrastructure is already ahead of all 7.

5. **The gifting angle is underserved by editorial sites.** VinePair covers it for reds only. Nobody covers "best wines to give as Christmas gifts" as a dedicated sub-section within a full Christmas wine guide — this is a high-intent, high-conversion angle.

6. **The "before/during/after" meal arc** (aperitif → white with starter → red with main → sweet with dessert → fizz for midnight) is gestured at by 8wines and Robb Report but never made explicit as a navigable structure.

## Novel Feature Recommendation

**Feature: "Christmas Dinner Timeline" strip with occasion-based filtering**

Specific build: A sticky `<StyleSelectorStrip>` (component already exists in codebase — `src/components/content/StyleSelectorStrip.astro`) adapted as a **5-stop occasion filter**:

```
[All] [Aperitif] [With Starter] [With Main] [With Dessert / Gifts]
```

Each wine listing carries a `data-wine-style` attribute matching one of the 5 stops. Clicking a pill hides non-matching wines.

**Why this is novel and shippable:**
- Zero of 7 competitors have any filtering mechanism — all are static lists
- The component already exists in the codebase; Lia just needs to add `data-wine-style` attributes and a `<StyleSelectorStrip>` call
- Directly answers the most common search intent: "I'm buying for a dinner — what do I serve at each course?"
- Distinguishes from our existing sangria and cheap-wine pages (neither uses occasion filtering)
- No new component build required from Dae — pure MDX integration

**Secondary recommendation (if Dae build time is available):**

A **"Gift or Drink?" toggle** at the top of the page — two modes:
- **Drink Tonight:** filters to dinner-pairing wines
- **Give as Gift:** filters to gift-appropriate bottles with gift-tier labels (Under $30 / $30–$60 / Splurge)

This directly captures the "wine for Christmas gift" and "Christmas wines" related searches identified in the "People also search for" data above. Nobody in the current SERP has this.
