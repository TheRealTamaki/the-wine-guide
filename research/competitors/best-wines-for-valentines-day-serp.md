## Structural Observations (What the SERP Rewards)

**1. Style-bucket H2 organisation universally present.** Every page that ranks uses H2 headings to bucket wines by type (Red / White / Rosé / Sparkling) or by mood/occasion. No page leads with a flat numbered list.

**2. Specific-bottle H3 headings beat variety-level H3s.** VinePair and Lexi's Wine List — the two most editorial, link-attracting pages — name the actual bottle in the H3. Your Wine Store and Wine Cellar HQ use variety names. Lia should go per-bottle.

**3. FAQ density is a ranking signal.** La Crema (6 Qs), Your Wine Store (6 Qs), Meadowcroft (5 Qs), Wine Cellar HQ (3 Qs) all have FAQ sections. Only VinePair and Lexi's skip it — and both are high-authority editorial domains where DA carries them. For The Wine Guide, a 5–7 question FAQ is mandatory.

**4. Zero pages include a romance-occasion matrix or style positioning tool.** Every page recommends wines by type. None maps wine style against occasion (date night vs. Galentine's vs. gifting vs. solo celebration) in a scannable, visual format.

**5. Chocolate pairing is a secondary content gap.** Multiple PAA questions reference chocolate pairing. Only Meadowcroft and Woo Girl have dedicated chocolate-pairing coverage. None of the per-bottle listicles address it.

**6. Lambrusco is a differentiation opportunity.** VinePair is the only page in the top 10 that includes Lambrusco — yet it is genuinely one of the most romantic, food-friendly styles and carries Valentine's Day credibility (festive, fizzy, red, approachable price). No advisory guide mentions it either.

## Novel Feature Recommendation

**"Romance Occasion Selector" — a 4-row, 5-column scannable matrix placed immediately after the intro, before TopPicksInline.**

Columns: Occasion | Wine Style | Why It Works | Best Bottle in This Guide | Approx. Price

Rows:
1. Date night dinner | Pinot Noir or Merlot | Versatile with food, mid-weight, crowd-safe | [linked wine from article] | $
2. Champagne toast moment | Brut or Blanc de Blancs | Ceremonial fizz, palate-resetting | [linked wine] | $$
3. Galentine's get-together | Rosé or Lambrusco | Light, fun, pours for a crowd | [linked wine] | $
4. Gifting (without meeting) | Port or dessert wine | Long-lived, sophisticated, conversation-starter | [linked wine] | $$

**Why it's shippable and novel:**
- Zero of the top 10 pages have an occasion-indexed table. Every page recommends by wine style, not by what the reader is actually doing on Valentine's Day.
- It is pure Markdown — no new component needed, ships with the first MDX draft.
- It directly absorbs PAA questions 6–10 (romantic dinner, gifting under $50, chocolate pairing, sparkling vs. red) in one structure.
- Lexi's and VinePair earn links for editorial depth — this table adds a decision-aid layer neither of them has.
- It mirrors the "Body + Occasion two-axis matrix" that shipped on `best-cheap-red-wine` and drew zero competitor equivalents.
